## Core Competencies

### Property Analysis Framework
1. **Yield Analysis**
   - Gross rental yield = Annual rent ÷ Purchase price × 100
   - Net rental yield = (Annual rent - Expenses) ÷ Purchase price × 100
   - Target: gross yield >4.5% Perth residential, >5.5% for cash flow positive

2. **Capital Growth Assessment**
   - Suburb growth drivers: infrastructure, population, employment, desirability
   - Perth market cycles — resource boom correlation, state economy
   - Supply/demand: vacancy rates, new supply pipeline, demolitions
   - Price-to-income ratio and affordability metrics

3. **Due Diligence Checklist**
   - Building inspection
   - Pest inspection
   - Strata report (if applicable)
   - Council zoning and development overlays
   - Flood/bushfire risk (Landgate WA)
   - Title search — encumbrances, easements, caveats
   - Rental appraisal from 3 agents

### Investment Structures

**Personal Name**
- Pros: simple, losses offset personal income (negative gearing)
- Cons: CGT on full net profit (50% discount after 12 months), no asset protection

**Discretionary Family Trust**
- Pros: income splitting among beneficiaries, asset protection, estate planning
- Cons: cannot negative gear (losses trapped in trust), set-up cost $1,500-$3,000
- Best for: positively geared or neutral properties, multiple beneficiaries

**SMSF Property**
- Pros: 15% tax on rental income, 10% CGT after 12 months, zero tax in pension phase
- Cons: must be arms-length, cannot live in it, limited recourse borrowing arrangement (LRBA) complexity
- Requires SMSF with $250,000+ before property makes sense
- Must be business real property for commercial SMSF purchases

**Company**
- Pros: 25-30% flat tax rate, asset protection
- Cons: no 50% CGT discount, unfranked dividends taxed at marginal rate
- Rarely optimal for investment property

### Financing Framework

**Borrowing Capacity Assessment**
- Rule of thumb: 4-5x gross income as maximum loan
- Stress test: can serviceability hold at interest rate +3%?
- Equity access: 80% LVR on existing property without LMI

**Loan Structuring**
- Interest-only vs principal and interest — depends on cash flow vs growth strategy
- Offset account vs redraw — offset preferred for flexibility
- Fixed vs variable — depends on rate environment and certainty needs
- Split loans for flexibility

**LVR Tiers**
- <60%: very low risk, maximum rate discounting
- 60-80%: standard residential lending
- 80-90%: LMI required, more expensive
- >90%: very difficult post-2023 lending restrictions

### Tax Optimisation for Property

**Negative Gearing**
- Losses from investment property reduce taxable income
- Best used when in 37% or 45% marginal tax bracket
- Depreciation schedule: quantity surveyor report to maximise deductions

**Depreciation**
- Division 43 (building): 2.5% of construction cost per year
- Division 40 (plant and equipment): hot water, carpets, appliances
- Quantity surveyor report cost: $600-$1,000 — usually worth it

**CGT Strategy**
- Hold >12 months for 50% discount
- Time disposals to a low-income year
- Offset gains against capital losses in portfolio
- Main residence exemption timing if property was ever PPOR
