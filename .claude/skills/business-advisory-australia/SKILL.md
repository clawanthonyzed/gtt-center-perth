## Core Business Knowledge

### Australian Business Structures

**Sole Trader**
- Simplest. Tax at personal marginal rate. Full personal liability.
- Best for: very early stage, under $75K revenue, testing ideas.
- ABN required. Tax file number used for returns.

**Company (PTY LTD)**
- Separate legal entity. Directors are personally liable only for insolvent trading.
- Tax rate: 25% (base rate entity with turnover <$50M, passive income <80%)
- Retaining profits in company taxed at 25% — better than 47% personal marginal rate
- Drawback: money is trapped unless paid as salary/dividend
- Franking credits accumulate when company pays tax — efficient dividend distribution
- Cost: $576 ASIC registration + annual review fee ~$310
- When to incorporate: when revenue exceeds $120,000-$150,000 or when liability risk increases

**Discretionary Family Trust + Corporate Trustee**
- Gold standard for Australian family business wealth
- Trustee company controls trust — protects personal assets
- Distribute income to family members, beneficiaries, other entities
- Losses trapped (not negative geared through)
- Setup: $2,000-$4,000 (solicitor + accountant + company registration)
- When to use: when the empire generates enough profit to benefit from income splitting

**Partnership**
- For two or more parties. Not relevant unless Anthony takes on partners.

**Combination Structure — Empire Optimal**
- Operating trust runs all 33 businesses
- Corporate trustee (PTY LTD) controls the trust
- Investment company holds shares and property
- Result: income splitting, asset protection, tax minimisation

### Venture Assessment Framework

Theodore uses this framework for every new project assessment:

**TAM / SAM / SOM**
- TAM (Total Addressable Market): worldwide market size
- SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): market we can actually reach
- SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): realistic share in 3 years

**Business Model Quality Score**
Rate each dimension 1-5:
1. Revenue predictability (recurring vs one-off)
2. Margin structure (>70% = excellent for digital)
3. Customer acquisition cost (low CAC = good)
4. Platform dependency risk (own audience vs platform)
5. Automation potential (can AI run this without Anthony?)

**Minimum Score to Proceed:** 15/25

**Kill Criteria (recommend terminating a project if):**
- No first revenue after 90 days and no clear unblocking path
- Revenue/effort ratio worse than existing Tier 1 projects
- Platform changes fundamentally alter the business model
- 3 consecutive QC failures with no improvement trend

### Scaling Playbook

When a project hits its revenue target:

**$1,000/month:**
- Validate the model. Don't scale yet. Understand why customers pay.
- Add social proof: screenshots, testimonials, case studies.

**$5,000/month:**
- Begin scaling the acquisition channel that's working.
- Start building an email list (owned audience > platform audience).
- Review pricing — can it be 20% higher without losing customers?

**$10,000/month:**
- Assess: what is the ceiling of this model at current structure?
- Consider: hire a human VA for repetitive tasks (content scheduling, customer replies)
- Consider: when does the manager need a human teammate?
- Tax: review whether to incorporate or restructure.

**$50,000/month:**
- Full structure review with Eleanor + Theodore.
- Consider: sell the business (SaaS businesses at 3-5x ARR, content businesses at 2-3x revenue)
- Consider: franchise model (boomer-and-roo has franchise potential)
- Consider: raise capital to accelerate (if organic growth is slow)

**$100,000/month+:**
- Board equivalent oversight warranted.
- External accountant required (Eleanor transitions to advisory, hands over compliance).
- IP holding structure, employment contracts, shareholder agreements.

### Risk Management Framework

**Risk Categories**
1. **Platform Risk**: business depends on Instagram/YouTube/Amazon/Gumroad policy
   - Mitigation: build owned channels (email lists) alongside every platform
   - Rule: no single platform >40% of any project's revenue

2. **Regulatory Risk**: government changes rules (GST, AI regulations, content laws)
   - Mitigation: follow Eleanor's compliance calendar. Stay ahead of ATO.
   - Monitor: AI regulation in Australia (currently light-touch)

3. **Technology Risk**: Claude API pricing changes, Anthropic policy changes
   - Mitigation: DOE architecture — execution layer can swap AI providers
   - Rule: no single API provider dependency for mission-critical workflows

4. **Concentration Risk**: too many projects in one sector
   - Current portfolio is well-diversified across 6 business types
   - Maintain: digital products, social, SaaS, commerce, services, finance all represented

5. **Cash Flow Risk**: empire has no revenue yet
   - Mitigation: bloom-and-bub is days from first sale (Gumroad unblock)
   - Rule: Tier 1 projects get 70% of agent time until first revenue
