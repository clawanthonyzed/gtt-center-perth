## 1. Accountant — Trust and Mixed-Supply Business Experience Required

**Why this venture specifically needs one, not a generic bookkeeper:** GTT Center Perth trades through YETI Holding Trust with a corporate trustee, has a mixed-supply GST profile (GST-free pathology component + GST-taxable wellness services in the same transaction), and Anthony's TPI pension means trust distribution structure matters for reasons beyond ordinary tax minimisation. A generalist accountant without discretionary-trust and mixed-supply-GST experience is a real risk here, not just a preference.

**When needed:** Week 1, before any other financial setup step — see `financial-setup.md` Step 1, which already exists as the briefing agenda for this exact conversation.

**Specific things to brief them on (already itemised in `financial-setup.md`):** trust/trustee structure, mixed supply GST apportionment method (flagged as "requires verification" in `pricing-billing-strategy.md`), the proposed 2028 minimum trust distribution tax and whether an operating PTY LTD is worth establishing ahead of it (`business-plan.md` §11), and default superannuation fund selection.

## 2. WA Small Business Development Corporation (SBDC) — Free Advisory

**Why this venture specifically could use it:** the SBDC offers free, government-funded advisory sessions for WA small businesses — business plan review, understanding lease obligations, and general startup guidance. For a venture at the pre-lease, pre-fit-out stage like this one, a free session reviewing the commercial lease heads of agreement before it's signed (or the business plan structure generally) is a low-cost sanity check alongside the paid solicitor/accountant work, not a replacement for it.

**When useful:** Anytime before the lease is signed — ideally alongside or before the commercial lease solicitor review (see §3 below), since the SBDC's guidance is free and general, while the solicitor is paid and specific to the actual lease document.

**Not previously mentioned anywhere in this corpus** — genuinely new resource, not a duplicate of anything already documented.

## 4. Insurance Broker — Health-Adjacent Premises Experience

**Why this venture specifically needs a broker with this experience, not a generic broker:** GTT Center Perth is a hybrid venue — part clinical (blood collection, fasting patients, potential adverse events) and part beauty/wellness (massage, nails, hair). A standard beauty-salon insurance policy likely won't adequately cover the clinical component (public liability exposure from a fainting/adverse-reaction incident during a blood draw is a different risk profile to a nail appointment), and a standard medical-clinic policy won't cover the wellness services. A broker experienced in health-adjacent premises (day spas with clinical add-ons, allied health practices with retail/wellness components) is the right fit.

**When needed:** Week 10, per the existing `financial-setup.md` Step 8 insurance checklist — this document already has the policy types and cost estimates (public liability A$20M, professional indemnity A$5M, workers compensation, commercial property). This section adds the "what kind of broker" detail that wasn't previously specified.

**Cross-reference:** `financial-setup.md` Step 8 for policy detail and cost estimates — not duplicated here.

## 5. Fair Work / HR Advisor

**Why this venture specifically needs one now, not just generic HR software:** this venture has already hit one real gap this session that a generalist HR template couldn't resolve — the MA000027 Saturday phlebotomist ordinary-hours question (`hr-framework.md`), which this session flagged as a genuine capability gap (no tool available to verify current award text). A Fair Work-specific advisor or the Fair Work Infoline (13 13 94) directly is the actual next step for that specific question, not a broader HR consultancy.

**When needed:** Alongside the Week 1 accountant/solicitor brief, and specifically before finalising phlebotomist employment contracts (since the Saturday ordinary-hours question affects their actual pay calculation).

**Relevant reference document (not previously cited in this corpus by name):** Fair Work Commission's Health Professionals and Support Services Award MA000027 — the actual award document is the primary source that needs checking, available at fairwork.gov.au. This session could not fetch it directly (no live web-fetch tool available) — flagged in `hr-framework.md` as a capability gap, not resolved by inventing a citation.
