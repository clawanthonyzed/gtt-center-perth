# GTT Center Perth — Afternoon Wellness Marketing Plan
## Standalone Demand Generation Strategy (12:00pm–5:00pm)
### Version 1.0 | June 2026 | Owner: Poppy (Marketing)

---

## WHY THIS DOCUMENT EXISTS

The main marketing plan (poppy-marketing.md) is built around midwife/OB referrals → GTT patients → morning shift. This works for morning GTT bookings.

**Afternoon patients are a completely different acquisition challenge:**
- They are NOT getting a GTT (no clinical referral required)
- They are standalone pregnancy wellness customers (massage, nails, brows, scan, dietitian)
- They discover GTT Center Perth through lifestyle channels: Instagram, Google, word-of-mouth
- They need a REASON to choose GTT Center Perth over any other pregnancy day spa in Perth

Without an afternoon-specific strategy, afternoon slots stay empty and the model loses A$19,250–26,950/month in projected revenue (Month 4+).

**Target:** 6–8 afternoon bookings/day from Month 2.

---

## 1. TARGET CUSTOMER — AFTERNOON WELLNESS

**She is different from the morning GTT patient:**

| Attribute | Morning GTT patient | Afternoon wellness patient |
|---|---|---|
| Motivation | Has a mandatory medical test | Wants to treat herself |
| Discovery | Midwife/OB refers her | Instagram, Google, friend recommends |
| Gestational age | 24–28 weeks (peak GTT window) | 20–36 weeks (broader window) |
| Repeat potential | Once (or twice for high-risk) | Monthly — she can come every month until birth |
| Urgency | Test is time-sensitive | Booking is discretionary |
| Decision maker | Referred — limited choice | Must be convinced — will compare options |

**Afternoon customer psychographic:**
- 28–38 years old, Perth metro (inner-west preferred)
- Household income A$100K+
- Already spending on prenatal yoga, pregnancy photography, maternity clothes
- Follows Perth pregnancy/parenting Instagram accounts
- Values experience and quality over pure price
- Plans ahead — books 1–2 weeks in advance
- Will share experiences on Instagram and within mum groups

## 2. PARTNER ACQUISITION CHANNELS (B2B → B2C)

These are referral partners who reach the afternoon customer that midwives don't.

|---|---|---|---|

| Physiotherapists (women's health) | Pelvic Floor First, Women's Health Perth | "Post-physio self-care — patients treating SPD/pelvic pain benefit from pregnancy massage." | 3–8 referrals/month per physio |
| OB/GYN who recommend massage | King Edward Memorial Hospital community OBs, private OBs | "We already serve many of your patients for GTT — our afternoon massage is a medical complement to your care plan." | 5–15 referrals/month per OB |
| Antenatal class providers | Hypnobirthing Australia Perth, private midwives running classes | "Your class participants need somewhere to relax and prepare. We offer a group booking rate for class cohorts." | 5–20 referrals per class cohort |
| Maternity wear shops | Bun Maternity, Cake Maternity stockists | Referral cards in fitting rooms | 2–5 leads/month |
| Pregnancy photographers | Perth bump photographers | Cross-referral: they recommend us for 'before the shoot' pampering | 3–8 referrals/month |
| Maternity hotels / birthing centres | Birth centres at SCGH, KEMH | "For families seeking premium birth preparation experiences" | Low volume but high intent |
| Doulas | Perth doula network | Doulas have intimate relationships with multiple clients simultaneously | 5–15 referrals/month per doula |

### Partnership Approach

**Week 1 of each partner outreach (Reed + Poppy coordinate):**
1. Personal visit or call from the Venue Manager — not just email
2. Offer: Venue Manager visits the studio/practice for a 20-minute introduction coffee
3. Leave: A5 referral card (premium printed), our menu card, business cards
4. Give: 2 complimentary afternoon visits (partner experiences the service, becomes a genuine advocate)
5. Follow up: 2 weeks later with a "how can we make this work for your clients?" call

**Formal partner offer (for top 10 partners):**
- 10% referral credit for every booking they send (tracked via Fresha "How did you hear about us?" field)
- OR: exclusive "partner pricing" for their clients (A$10 off first visit)
- Quarterly check-in: Venue Manager visits the top 5 partners in person every 3 months

- **Not yet actioned:** exact ad copy, landing page content, and Instagram caption templates featuring specific package names/prices — held pending Anthony's sign-off on `pm-package-structure.md`'s proposed pricing.

## 6. GROUP BOOKING STRATEGY

Group bookings fill multiple afternoon slots in one sale. High efficiency.

**Target groups:**
- Antenatal class cohorts (6–10 women at the same gestational stage)
- Work baby shower groups (colleagues celebrating a pregnancy — 4–6 people, mix of pregnant + non-pregnant)
- Mother-daughter visits (mum accompanies pregnant daughter — 2 bookings)

**Group offer:**
- 6+ bookings from one group: 10% discount across the group
- Group organiser gets a complimentary snack platter (A$25 cost, high perceived value)
- Exclusive hire of the lounge for 2 hours (makes it feel special)

**Promote via:** Instagram, partner referrals, waitlist email sequences.

## 7. POSTNATAL MARKET (Month 6 — soft expansion)

The afternoon shift is perfectly positioned for postnatal customers (new mothers, 0–12 months post-birth). Same staff, same venue, minor service adjustments.

**Month 6 launch:**
- Add "Postnatal Massage" as a service option in Fresha for afternoon slots
- 1–2 postnatal massage slots per afternoon (does not reduce pregnancy slots — uses underutilised capacity)
- Pricing: A$120–150 for 60-min postnatal massage
- Marketing: "Our venue is for the whole journey — from bump to baby"

**Why this matters:**
- GTT patients who become postnatal customers = zero acquisition cost (warm relationship)
- Postnatal referrals are powerful — new mums share recommendations intensely
- Revenue: if 30 GTT patients per month convert to postnatal customers in Month 6+ = +A$3,600–4,500/month

## 8. AFTERNOON BOOKING FUNNEL

| Stage | Tool | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Instagram, Google, partner referrals | Educational + lifestyle content |
| Interest | Instagram link in bio → Afternoon page on website | Service descriptions, pricing, photos |
| Decision | Website → Fresha booking | Clear CTAs, social proof (Google reviews), booking ease |
| Booking | Fresha | Standard booking flow (no GTT constraint needed) |
| Confirmation | Fresha auto-email → Resend sequence | Welcome email, what to expect |
| Post-visit | Resend post-visit sequence | Google review request, referral offer, next booking suggestion |
| Retention | Fresha "suggest rebooking" | "You visited X weeks ago — time for another?" |

**Afternoon email sequence (distinct from morning GTT sequence):**
- Confirmation: "Your afternoon at GTT Center Perth is booked. Here's what to expect..."
- 24hr reminder: "See you tomorrow at [time]. Arrive as you are — we'll do the rest."
- Day-of: "Your appointment is today at [time]. Free parking at [location]."
- Post-visit (24hr): "How was your afternoon? [Google review link] | [Book again link]"
- 6-week nudge: "It's been 6 weeks since your last visit. Feeling ready for another hour to yourself?"
