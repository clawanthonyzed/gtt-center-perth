## Critical Finding: TPI Pension is NOT Means Tested

**The TPI Disability Compensation Payment (Special Rate) under the Veterans Entitlements Act 1986 is NOT means tested.**

This is the most important finding. Specifically:
- NOT subject to an income test
- NOT subject to an assets test
- Non-taxable income
- Paid for life regardless of Anthony's income, assets, or business involvement
- Trust income, business income, or Imara's income does NOT threaten the core TPI payment

**Source:** DVA official guidance: "The disability compensation payment is not means tested."

## However — The Critical Unknown: Does Anthony Also Hold a Service Pension?

Many TPI veterans receive BOTH:
1. **TPI Disability Compensation Payment** — not means tested (SAFE regardless of trust income)
2. **Service Pension** — means tested (trust income CAN affect this)

**This must be confirmed immediately.** If Anthony holds a Service Pension or Invalidity Service Pension, DVA trust attribution rules become relevant and could reduce that payment if business income is attributed to him.

**Action required:** Check Anthony's DVA entitlements letter or myGov DVA account to confirm which payments he receives.

## Trust Income and DVA — Full Analysis

### If Anthony Holds TPI Only (No Service Pension)
- Trust income = ZERO effect on TPI
- Imara receiving trust distributions = ZERO effect on TPI
- Anthony being director of corporate trustee = ZERO effect on TPI
- **Conclusion: Proceed with YETI Trust structure with no DVA risk**

### If Anthony Holds Service Pension (In Addition to TPI)
Service Pension IS means tested. DVA applies trust attribution rules:

**Control test:** A person "controls" a trust if they can influence trustee decisions or benefit from the trust. Being director of the corporate trustee (YETI Tipi Holdings PTY LTD) almost certainly makes Anthony an "attributable stakeholder."

**If attributed:** DVA treats the trust's full income as Anthony's income for Service Pension calculation. This could reduce or cancel Service Pension.

**Couples rule:** DVA assesses combined couple income. Imara's trust distributions would be counted as part of the couple's income, even if Imara physically receives them.

**Mitigation options (to discuss with DVA adviser):**
1. Remove Anthony from YETI Tipi Holdings PTY LTD directorship — Imara becomes sole director
2. Review YETI trust deed to ensure Anthony has no formal powers
3. Document that Anthony has no operational role in the business
4. Note: DVA also tests for INFORMAL control — formal removal alone may not be sufficient

## SRDP vs TPI

**SRDP (Special Rate Disability Pension)** = MRCA equivalent of VEA TPI
- Both compensate veterans who cannot work due to service injury
- Both are NOT means tested
- VETS Act (effective 1 July 2026): new TPI-equivalent claims go through MRCA as SRDP; Anthony's existing TPI payment continues unchanged under VEA rules

**If Anthony is on TPI (VEA):** Nothing changes on 1 July 2026. Existing payment continues.

## The "Cannot Work" Eligibility Condition — Important Caution

The TPI/SRDP pension requires that Anthony's service-related condition makes him unable to work:
- **VEA "alone test":** Service injury ALONE prevents working
- **MRCA test (if applicable):** Service conditions contribute to inability to work more than 10 hours/week

**Risk if Anthony is perceived as actively managing a business:**
If DVA receives information suggesting Anthony is functioning as a business manager/director, they could trigger a re-assessment of whether he still meets the "cannot work" test.

**Mitigation:**
- Anthony's role as director of YETI Tipi Holdings PTY LTD should be **nominal and administrative only**
- All operational business decisions documented as Imara's decisions
- Anthony's medical evidence of incapacity maintained with current specialist reports
- "I signed a company document" is materially different from "I manage daily operations" — keep it that way

**Strong recommendation:** Have a DVA Veterans' Affairs lawyer (not a general solicitor) review the directorship arrangement before signing anything.

## Recent DVA Changes (2024-2026)

- **VETS Act (Feb 2025, effective 1 July 2026):** Existing TPI payments continue unchanged. New claims go through MRCA/SRDP pathway. No change to income/assets test rules for existing TPI holders.
- **Deeming rates (March 2026):** 1.25% under $64,200 (single) / $106,200 (couple); 3.25% above. Applies to Service Pension financial asset assessment only.
- **Asset limits (1 July 2025):** $321,500 homeowner single; $481,500 couple — applies to Service Pension, not TPI.
