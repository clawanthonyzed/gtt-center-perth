# GTT Center Perth — Pathology Collection Room: Creation Guide

**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-06-09

---

## Purpose

This document details exactly how to design, fit out, equip, and maintain the pathology collection room at GTT Center Perth. Compliance with ACSQHC *Guidelines for Approved Pathology Collection Centres* (3rd Ed 2013) and NATA ISO 15189 requirements is mandatory for Option B (own ACC). Option A (PathWest/WDP satellite) uses the same room specification — the pathology provider will inspect before agreeing to operate from the venue.

---

## Room Specifications

### Dimensions and Location
- **Minimum floor area:** 9 sqm (3m x 3m)
- **Recommended:** 12-14 sqm to allow wheelchair maneuverability and multiple bays
- **Location in venue:** Adjacent to reception/entry — patients reach collection room WITHOUT passing through treatment rooms
- **Signage:** "Pathology Collection — By Appointment" on door (avoid clinical language that may alarm patients)
- **Door:** Minimum 850mm clear width (DDA wheelchair access)

### Privacy
- Dedicated enclosed room — not an open bay
- Solid door (not just a curtain)
- Soundproofed if possible (patients may feel anxious; privacy increases calm)
- Window (natural light preferred) with frosted glass or blinds for patient privacy

## Construction and Fit-Out Requirements

### Flooring
- **Material:** Commercial-grade vinyl or tiles — non-slip, seamless (no grout lines to trap blood), chemically resistant
- No carpet (cannot be adequately decontaminated after blood spill)
- Coved skirting (floor meets wall with curved cove — no gap for fluid ingress, easy mopping)

### Walls
- Light-coloured paint (easy to see contamination)
- Washable, moisture-resistant surface (semi-gloss minimum)
- No soft furnishings, fabric panels, or artwork that cannot be wiped down

### Ceiling
- Smooth, wipeable surface
- No exposed air conditioning vents directly above collection area (aerosol risk)

### Lighting
- **Minimum:** 400 lux at work surface (vein visibility)
- **Recommended:** 600-800 lux (LED, no shadows)
- Adjustable task lighting over phlebotomy chair preferred
- LED with CRI > 85 (shows true colour — important for patient assessment)

### Ventilation
- Adequate air change rate — minimum 6 ACH (air changes per hour)
- MUST NOT recirculate air through shared HVAC without HEPA filtration
- Separate exhaust fan strongly recommended — ducted to outside
- Air conditioning acceptable if dedicated unit (not shared return air system)

### Plumbing
- **Hand hygiene station at room entry** — non-negotiable per NSQHS Standards
  - Basin with **elbow-operated taps** (or sensor taps) — hands-free operation required
  - Liquid soap dispenser (wall-mounted)
  - Paper towel dispenser (not cloth towels)
  - ABHR (alcohol-based hand rub) wall-mounted dispenser — separate from soap
  - Splashback: stainless steel or tiled, fully sealed

### Power and Data
- **Minimum 4 power outlets** in room (phlebotomy chair lighting, centrifuge, label printer, device charging)
- All outlets at bench height minimum (avoid floor-level in clinical wet environment)
- Data point (RJ45 or WiFi access point) for electronic patient identification system

## Essential Equipment List

### Phlebotomy Chair
- Reclining, with adjustable arm support (extend to full horizontal for patients with needle phobia/vasovagal risk)
- Non-fabric upholstery (vinyl, wipe-clean)
- Wide seat (bariatric options available — relevant in pregnancy context)
- **Perth suppliers:** Sunflower Medical, Alphatec Australia, Ultramedic
- **Cost:** A$600-1,500 per chair
- **Quantity:** 2, independent of Option A/B — required to meet the 8-client/morning capacity model (2 chairs, 2 phlebotomists, alternating-client assignment: Chair A takes odd-numbered clients, Chair B takes even-numbered). See operations-manual.md for the verified per-client and per-staff timetable.

### Tabletop Centrifuge
- **Critical for GDM accuracy:** NaF/fluoride-oxalate tubes MUST be centrifuged within 10 minutes of draw to prevent glycolysis (false low glucose reading — causes 62% GDM underdiagnosis per WA ORCHID study)
- Minimum spec: 3,000 RPM minimum, 8+ position rotor, refrigerated preferred
- **Recommended models:**
  - Hettich EBA 200 (basic, ~A$1,800)
  - Hettich Zentrifuge Universal 320R (refrigerated, ~A$3,500)
  - Thermo Scientific CL10 (A$2,000-3,000)
  - Chinese equivalent (Hunan Xiangyi, REMI) — A$400-800 direct, verify NATA acceptance before purchasing
- **Cost:** A$1,800-5,000
- **Quantity:** 1 (maintain backup ice slurry protocol in QMS if centrifuge fails) — serves both chairs. Chair A and Chair B draws are offset by 10-20min in the verified timetable, so both chairs' specimens reach the centrifuge within the 10-minute window without contention.

### Sharps Containers
- Must comply with **AS/NZS 23907:2023** (puncture-resistant, leak-proof, biohazard labelled)
- **Placement:** Elbow-height — phlebotomist deposits needle without reaching up or down
- Wall-mounted bracket strongly preferred (prevents tipping)
- Sizes: 1.4L (bench) and 5L (room)
- **Cost:** A$5-15 each (consumable — replace when 3/4 full, never compress)
- **Supplier:** Daniels Health, Cleanaway Medical, Stericycle

### Biohazard Waste System
- Yellow biohazard bins with labelled liners for clinical waste (blood-contaminated gloves, gauze, cotton balls)
- Sharps container separate from clinical waste
- **Medical waste contractor (Perth):** Cleanaway, Stericycle, Veolia — contract BEFORE opening
- Collection frequency: weekly minimum for active venue
- **Cost:** A$50-100/month disposal contract

### Specimen Transport
- Insulated specimen transport bag (for courier to APA)
- Biohazard specimen pouches (ziplock, separate compartment for requisition slip)
- Freezer brick/ice pack for cold chain (NaF tubes — keep cool, not frozen)

### Patient Identification
- Most APAs supply their own barcode labels — confirm with PathWest/WDP before purchasing separately
- Back-up: manual 2-identifier system (name + DOB, confirmed verbally by patient before every draw)
- Wristband printer (Zebra LP2844 or equivalent) if APA does not supply pre-printed labels

### Documentation Station
- Small desk or bench (900mm wide minimum)
- Clipboard system OR tablet for patient consent forms
- Secure lockable drawer for patient documentation (Privacy Act 1988 compliance)

## Secondary Equipment

### Emergency Response
- Emergency call button (wall-mounted, connected to reception desk alert)
- First aid kit accessible within 30 seconds — minimum: smelling salts, bandages, gloves, CPR mask
- Chair reclines to FULLY FLAT — mandatory for vasovagal management (patient who faints must lie fully flat)
- Phone or panic button connected to reception

### Ice Slurry Station (Backup for Centrifuge Failure)
- Polystyrene or insulated cooler box
- Ice packs
- Protocol: if centrifuge unavailable, place tubes in ice slurry immediately post-draw, transport to APA within 30 min
- This is the **backup protocol only** — centrifuge must be on-site and operational

### Consumables Storage (Lockable)
- Small lockable cabinet for: vacuum blood collection tubes, needles, tourniquets, alcohol wipes, gauze, cotton balls, gloves, labels, requisition forms
- Consumables typically supplied by APA under Option A (confirm in agreement)
- Under Option B: venue purchases own consumables

## Cleaning and Infection Control

### Between Each Patient (5 minutes)
1. Remove and dispose of all sharps, contaminated items into biohazard waste
2. Wipe chair arm support with TGA-listed disinfectant (e.g. Viraclean, Clinicide)
3. Wipe all touched surfaces (bench, chair armrest, door handle)
4. Replace fresh paper roll on chair (if used)
5. Hand hygiene: full 6-step technique with ABHR

### End of Day
1. Deep clean entire room with TGA-listed hospital-grade disinfectant
2. Mop floor
3. Empty and replace biohazard bin liners
4. Check sharps container fill level — replace if more than 3/4 full
5. Check temperature log (room and cold storage)
6. Restock consumables for next day
7. Record cleaning in daily log

### Spill Management
- Blood spill kit: absorbent granules + TGA-listed disinfectant + heavy-duty waste bag + gloves
- Spill kit stored IN the collection room
- Procedure: absorb, decontaminate, dispose as clinical waste, report in incident log

## Patient Pathway (Timeline)

```
T=0:   Patient arrives at reception
       Identity confirmed (2 identifiers: name + DOB)
T+2:   Phlebotomist walks patient to collection room
T+3:   Patient seated in phlebotomy chair, arm positioned
       Phlebotomist performs 2-identifier check again (safety step)
T+8:   Fasting blood draw complete (2-3 tubes) — total 5-8 minutes
T+13:  Glucose drink complete (75g in 250-300mL water, patient finishes within 5 min)
T+15:  Timer started. Patient exits collection room to lounge area.
       [TOTAL TIME IN COLLECTION ROOM: 10-15 minutes]
       [SERVICES CAN BEGIN FROM T+15]
T+75:  Patient returns for 1-hour draw (5 minutes)
T+135: Patient returns for 2-hour draw (5 minutes) — FINAL DRAW
       Specimens labelled, centrifuged, couriered to APA same day
```

**Key insight — service scheduling:** Patients can begin beauty/wellness services from T+15. They remain seated or reclined ("at rest") throughout — compatible with GTT protocol. Services should be scheduled to end by T+110 (10-minute buffer before 2-hour draw).
