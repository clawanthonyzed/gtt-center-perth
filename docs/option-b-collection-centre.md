# GTT Center Perth — Option B: Own Approved Collection Centre

**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-06-09 | **Status:** Planning Phase

---

## Overview

Option B means GTT Center Perth operates its own Medicare-approved pathology collection facility. This replaces the Option A model (hosting a PathWest/WDP satellite collection point) and gives the venture full operational control, full Medicare billing authority, and the ability to employ its own phlebotomist.

**HARD RULE (unchanged):** No in-house glucose analysis. Blood is collected in-house; specimens are couriered to PathWest or WDP for analysis. Results go directly to the referring GP. GTT Center Perth never communicates clinical results to patients.

---

## Why Pursue Option B

| Factor | Option A (Partner) | Option B (Own ACC) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Control | Low — pathology provider dictates hours, staff, room access | Full control of hours, staffing, room design |
| Revenue | Zero from pathology | Negotiated ACC fee (A$5-15 per specimen) |
| Branding | Pathology provider brand on-site | Full GTT Center Perth brand experience |
| Flexibility | Dependent on provider schedule | Set your own schedule, accept walk-ins |
| Cost | Near-zero operational cost | Ongoing compliance, staff, accreditation costs |
| Timeline | Fast (weeks) | 6-9 months minimum |

**Recommendation:** Launch with Option A (fastest to market), run in parallel with Option B accreditation application, switch to Option B at month 6-9.

## Step-by-Step Process: Becoming an Approved Collection Centre

### Step 1 — Understand the Regulatory Framework

**Key documents (must obtain and read):**
- ACSQHC *Guidelines for Approved Pathology Collection Centres: Requirements for Medical Pathology Specimen Collection* (3rd Edition, 2013) — free download from ACSQHC website
- NATA ISO 15189:2022 Medical Laboratory Accreditation requirements
- Medicare Benefits Schedule — pathology collection items (Items 73070-73340)
- Health Insurance Act 1973 (Cth) — ss.16B, 23DNBA definitions of Approved Pathology Authority and Approved Collection Centre

**Regulatory chain:**
```
GTT Center Perth (Approved Collection Centre)
    <--> contract/agreement
Approved Pathology Authority (PathWest or WDP)
    <--> Medicare billing
Services Australia (pays pathology for each test)
```

GTT Center Perth as an ACC collects specimens but CANNOT bill Medicare independently. Only the Approved Pathology Authority (PathWest/WDP) bills Medicare. Revenue sharing is negotiated between ACC and APA.

---

### Step 2 — NATA Accreditation (ISO 15189)

**What it is:** National Association of Testing Authorities accreditation to ISO 15189:2022 (Medical Laboratories). Mandatory for any entity collecting human specimens intended for diagnostic purposes billed to Medicare.

**Timeline:** 4-6 months from application to accreditation (if first attempt)

**Process:**
1. Register on NATA portal (nata.com.au) — create applicant account
2. Complete Scope of Accreditation application — specify "Pathology collection only (non-testing)" scope
3. Submit site information: floor plan, equipment list, QMS documentation, staff qualifications
4. Initial document review (6-8 weeks)
5. On-site assessment by NATA assessors (schedule ~3 months after submission)
6. Gap report issued — address any non-conformances
7. Technical review panel approval
8. Certificate issued

**Estimated fees (2026):**
- Application fee: A$2,000-2,500
- Annual accreditation fee: A$1,500-3,000/year depending on scope
- Assessor costs (travel, time): A$1,000-2,000 for Perth assessment
- **Total initial cost: A$4,500-7,500**

**Key ISO 15189 requirements for collection-only scope:**
- Documented Quality Management System (QMS) — minimum 15 core procedures
- Pre-examination procedures: patient identification, sample labelling, specimen integrity
- Personnel requirements: qualifications, training records, competency assessments
- Equipment: calibration records for centrifuge, cold storage (ice slurry), thermometers
- Environmental controls: room temperature monitoring, waste management
- Non-conformance and incident reporting system
- Internal audit schedule (annual minimum)

**Who writes the QMS?** A quality consultant can write ISO 15189 QMS documentation for A$5,000-15,000. Alternatively, purchase a template QMS (A$500-2,000) and customise. Cora (clinical agent) manages ongoing QMS once live.

---

### Step 3 — Medicare Approval as Approved Collection Centre

**Authority:** Services Australia (Department of Health and Aged Care)
**Application portal:** PRODA / HPOS (Health Professional Online Services)
**Timeline:** Up to 90 days (3 months) from submission; submit BEFORE intended trading date

**Eligibility requirements (must be met BEFORE application):**
1. NATA accreditation for relevant scope — certificate must be current
2. Written agreement with at least one Approved Pathology Authority (PathWest or WDP)
3. Facility meets ACSQHC Collection Centre Guidelines (3rd Ed)
4. Personnel meet qualification requirements (see Step 4)

**Application documents required:**
- NATA accreditation certificate (current)
- Copy of agreement with Approved Pathology Authority
- Facility details: address, contact, hours of operation
- Nominated person-in-charge (PIC) details and qualifications
- Evidence of staff qualifications (phlebotomist credentials)

**Person In Charge (PIC) requirements:**
- Must be named individual responsible for compliance
- Qualifications: Certificate IV in Pathology Collection (HLT37215) OR registered health professional (RN, doctor, etc.)
- PIC can be same person as the phlebotomist

**Application form:** HW056 (Approved Pathology Authority application) — download from Services Australia website

---

### Step 4 — Hire Qualified Phlebotomist / PIC

**Minimum qualification:** Certificate III in Pathology Collection (HLT33015) — sufficient for collection
**Preferred qualification:** Certificate IV in Pathology Collection (HLT37215) — required to be PIC

**WA training providers:**
- Pathology Training Australia (online + practical placement)
- TAFE WA — allied health programs
- Practical component: minimum 35 hours supervised clinical practice (must be arranged at existing accredited collection centre — contact PathWest or Laverty for placement)

**Timeline for new phlebotomist to qualify from scratch:** 6-12 months (Cert III) or up to 18 months (Cert IV)

**Hire a qualified phlebotomist instead (faster path):**
Perth phlebotomist salary:
- Casual: A$35-42/hr
- Part-time (25hrs/week): ~A$48,000-55,000/year base
- Add 12% super: ~A$54,000-62,000/year total cost

**Where to recruit:**
- Seek.com.au (search "phlebotomist Perth")
- PathWest alumni (LinkedIn — many interested in smaller venues)
- Healthscope, Australian Clinical Labs — staff sometimes seek lifestyle change
- Allied health recruitment agencies: Medacs, Pulse Staffing

**Key hire requirement:** Phlebotomist MUST hold credentials acceptable to the APA (PathWest/WDP). Confirm before extending offer.

---

### Step 5 — Facility Compliance

**ACSQHC Guidelines for Collection Centres (3rd Ed 2013) — Key Requirements:**

**Physical environment:**
- Dedicated pathology collection room (not shared with other services during collection)
- Minimum 9 sqm floor area
- Wheelchair access (DDA compliant)
- Hand hygiene station (ABHR + liquid soap) at room entry
- Natural or artificial ventilation — no aerosol recirculation risk
- Secure, lockable storage for consumables and biohazard waste

See pathology-collection-room.md for full fit-out specification.

---

### Step 6 — Agreement with Approved Pathology Authority

**Who to approach:** PathWest (public) and Western Diagnostic Pathology (WDP, private) — approach both simultaneously.

**Negotiation points:**
- Pick-up schedule: WDP runs 2x daily couriers in Perth metro — confirm GTT Center Perth is on route
- Turnaround time: GTT results typically 24-48 hours to referring GP
- Specimen rejection protocol: what happens if a tube is compromised?
- Results communication: confirm results go DIRECTLY to referring GP only (GTT Center Perth never informed)
- Fee arrangement: negotiate a collection centre service fee (A$5-15 per specimen is industry norm for high-volume sites)
- Consumables supply: most APAs supply vacuum blood tubes, labels, sharps containers at no cost to ACC (confirm in agreement)

**Specialist legal review:** The APA agreement must be reviewed by a health law solicitor before signing. Cost: A$500-1,500. This is not optional — incorrect agreements create Medicare audit risk.

---

### Step 7 — Ongoing Compliance

**Annual requirements:**
- NATA surveillance assessment (typically annual on-site or desktop review)
- Internal audits of QMS (minimum quarterly recommended)
- Staff competency assessments (annual minimum per ISO 15189)
- Equipment calibration records (centrifuge, thermometers)
- Incident reporting to NATA if significant non-conformances

**Medicare compliance:**
- Services Australia can audit any ACC at any time
- Maintain records for 7 years (Medicare audit standard)
- Notify Services Australia of any changes to PIC, address, hours, or APA agreement

**Costs summary (ongoing, annual):**
- NATA accreditation fee: A$1,500-3,000/year
- Medical waste disposal: A$50-100/month (A$600-1,200/year)
- QMS maintenance (part of phlebotomist role): included in wages
- Consumables (typically supplied by APA — verify in agreement)

## Key Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| NATA assessment fails first attempt | Medium | High (3-6 month delay) | Engage NATA-accredited consultant from day 1 |
| Phlebotomist hire difficulty in Perth | Medium | High | Begin recruitment in parallel with NATA process |
| APA refuses to partner with ACC | Low | Critical | Approach both PathWest and WDP; have legal agreement template ready |
| Medicare audit | Low | High | Maintain all records 7 years; full QMS compliance |
| Centrifuge failure post-draw | Low | High | Keep backup ice slurry protocol; emergency centrifuge hire contact |
