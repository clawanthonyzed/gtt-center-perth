## Scenario D (15 clients, 3rd phlebotomist) — re-run as requested

**Important finding, correcting the earlier claim that multi-role would make the 3rd phlebotomist "more attractive":** at Scenario D volume, Massage and Beauty *individually* both already peak at 2 concurrent each — cross-training them only helps when their peaks don't coincide (true at today's 10-client volume, not true at 15-client volume, where they peak together). So **the multi-role saving (8→7) only exists at today's volume. If the 3rd phlebotomist is added and volume grows to 15/day, treatment headcount goes back up to 8** — the full complement, same as today's uncrossed model, no further reduction available at that scale.

**This actually confirms — not weakens — the original Scenario D finding** in `am-capacity-weekend.md`: *"no new treatment-staff hire required"* for the 3rd chair. 8 total treatment staff (cross-trained or not) comfortably covers 15 clients/day. Multi-role's real benefit is squeezing today's 10-client operation from 8 down to 7 — it's not an argument either for or against the 3rd phlebotomist specifically, that decision still rests entirely on the WDP cutoff/window question.

## Recommendation

Use **7 staff** at current volume (Massage+Beauty pool of 3 dual-qualified people, 2 standalone Nails, 2 standalone Hair). If/when the 3rd phlebotomist is added, plan to add 1 more Massage/Beauty-qualified person back (7→8) to hold the Scenario D peak — not a surprise cost, just worth having on record now rather than discovering it later.
