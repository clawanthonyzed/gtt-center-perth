## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

GTT Center Perth is Western Australia's first premium wellness venue built around the Glucose Tolerance Test (GTT) — a mandatory 2.5-hour pregnancy screening test that ~14,400 Perth women undergo every year. Instead of sitting in a bare pathology waiting room while fasting, patients book into GTT Center Perth and spend their test window receiving massage, nails, brows, a 3D keepsake scan, or a dietitian consultation.

The venue runs a **morning GTT shift** (3 patient waves: 7:30am–12:15pm) and an **afternoon wellness shift** (12:00pm–5:00pm, no testing). Practitioners are employed directly. The phlebotomist operates under WDP's NATA accreditation as a Licensed Collection Centre.

**The only equivalent business in Australia is MIWM in Melbourne** — fully booked 3–4 weeks in advance at A$365 per visit. Perth is entirely unserved.

**Target:** 12–15 visits/day. Break-even at ~108 visits/month. Profitable from Month 3.

## 2. PROBLEM

### The GTT Experience Today
1. Pregnant women receive a pathology request for a GTT at 24–28 weeks
2. They book at PathWest or WDP — bare clinical waiting room
3. They arrive fasting (8–14 hours without food)
4. They sit for 2.5 hours: waiting, nothing to do, physically uncomfortable
5. They leave tired, hungry, and having wasted half a morning

**This happens ~277 times per week in Perth metro.** There is no alternative.

### The Market Gap
- Zero wellness-integrated GTT venues in WA
- Zero venues in Perth targeting the captive GTT waiting window
- MIWM Melbourne: the only equivalent nationally — fully booked months in advance
- Perth's maternal wellness market is underserved relative to east coast

---

## 3. SOLUTION

GTT Center Perth is a purpose-built venue where:
- The GTT blood draws happen on-site (phlebotomist employed, under WDP NATA umbrella)
- The 2.5-hour wait is filled with premium wellness services

## 5. OPERATIONS

### Shift Structure

#### Morning Shift (GTT + Services)
3 patient waves, staggered 30-minute start slots:

| Wave | GTT Starts | Patients per wave | T=120 done |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wave 1 | 7:30am, 8:00am | 2 | 9:30–10:15am |
| Wave 2 | 8:30am, 9:00am | 2 | 10:30–11:15am |
| Wave 3 | 9:30am, 10:00am | 1 | 11:30am–12:15pm |
| **Total morning** | | **5 GTT patients max (1 phlebotomist)** | |

GTT must start ≤10:00am (ADIPS 2014 guideline — circadian glucose tolerance constraint).
**Capacity note:** 5 is the safe maximum with 1 phlebotomist due to draw overlap at 9:30am. Second phlebotomist (Month 2–3) unlocks 8 patients/morning.

#### Afternoon Shift (Wellness Only)
12:00pm–5:00pm. No testing. Same staff continue.
- 6–8 wellness appointments (massage, nails, brows, scan, dietitian)

#### Daily Capacity
- Morning GTT: 5 patients (1 phlebotomist) / 8 patients (2 phlebotomists from Month 2–3)
- Afternoon wellness: 6–8 patients (requires full-time staff — see staff-plan.md)
- **Total: 11–16 visits/day**

## 7. COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

| Competitor | Location | Model | Price | Availability |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| MIWM (Maternal & Infant Wellbeing Melbourne) | Parkville, VIC | Integrated GTT + spa | A$365 | Booked 3–4 weeks out |
| PathWest / WDP collection centres | Perth | Clinical pathology only | Bulk-billed | No wait time management |
| Perth pregnancy day spas | Various | Wellness only (no GTT) | A$100–200/treatment | Available |
| Individual midwife practices | Perth | Clinical only | N/A | No wellness component |

**Key competitive advantages:**
1. **First mover in WA:** Zero direct competition
2. **Captive audience:** Patients are already committed to the 2.5-hour wait — not discretionary
3. **Morning monopoly:** GTT timing creates a guaranteed morning pipeline (2,165+ patients/year addressable)
4. **Referral compounding:** Each midwife/OB referral = ongoing stream of captive customers
5. **Proven model:** MIWM has operated this profitably in Melbourne for years, fully booked
6. **Low substitutability:** Patients cannot do their GTT elsewhere and come here for services separately (they'd have to leave the premises — not permitted during GTT)

## 12. PROPERTY STRATEGY

### Can YETI Trust purchase commercial property and GTT Center Perth rent from it?

**YES — this is a valid and tax-efficient structure.**

**How it works:**
1. YETI Holding Trust purchases commercial property (warehouse or retail site)
2. GTT Center Perth (trading as trust, OR as separate operating company) pays rent to the trust
3. Rental income = trust income (distributed to beneficiaries at their tax rate)
4. Rent = deductible business expense for GTT Center Perth
5. Property appreciates in trust — CGT discount (50%) applies if held > 12 months

**Financial requirements:**
- Commercial property purchase: 20–30% deposit required by lender
- Perth metro commercial strata (150–200 sqm): A$500,000–900,000 depending on suburb
- Deposit required: A$100,000–270,000
- Monthly commercial mortgage (P&I, 20yr, ~7.5% commercial rate): A$4,000–7,500/month

**Tax advantage:**
- GTT Center Perth deducts rent at operating company/trust tax rate
- Trust receives rental income distributed at beneficiary rates
- If property held > 12 months and sold: 50% CGT discount on capital gain

**Recommendation:**
- **Year 1:** Lease (standard commercial lease — confirm model works before committing capital)
- **Year 2–3:** If model is proven, trust purchases commercial property in right Perth location
- **Structure:** YETI Trust purchases → GTT Center Perth pays rent back to trust → generates passive income stream separate from operating income

*Note: This is general knowledge, not specific legal or financial advice. Solicitor and accountant must advise on specific transaction before any purchase.*
