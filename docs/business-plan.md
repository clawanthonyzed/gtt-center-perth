# GTT Center Perth — Business Plan

**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-06-04 | **Status:** Pre-revenue / STANDBY

---

## Venture Summary

GTT Center Perth is WA's first dedicated waiting experience for pregnant women undergoing the Glucose Tolerance Test (GTT). Most pregnant women between 24–28 weeks spend 2–3 hours at pathology for this mandatory screening. GTT Center Perth fills that wait with massage, nails, hair, and 3D keepsake ultrasound — all co-located with or adjacent to a pathology collection point.

**Validated east-coast precedent:** Maternal & Infant Wellbeing Melbourne (MIWM, Parkville) charges A$365/visit, is booked 3–4 weeks in advance, and has operated this model profitably. WA market is entirely unserved.

---

## Problem

- Pregnant women wait 2–3 hours for GTT results
- Wait is in a bare pathology waiting room: fasting, uncomfortable, nothing to do
- WA has zero equivalent services (MIWM is Melbourne only)
- East-coast demand is proven — Perth has same population of pregnant women

---

## Solution

A purpose-built booking experience that pairs a GTT appointment with on-site premium services:

| Service | Delivery Model |
|---|---|
| Pregnancy massage | Sublet room to self-employed therapist |
| Nails (mani/pedi) | Sublet chair to self-employed nail tech |
| Hair (blowdry/treatment) | Sublet chair to self-employed hairdresser |
| 3D keepsake ultrasound | Sublet scan room to existing Perth operator (e.g. Cedar 4D, Oh Hey Baby) |
| Pathology blood draw | Partner with PathWest or WDP — mobile/satellite collection point |

---

## Target Customer

- Pregnant women, 24–28 weeks, Perth metro
- ~18,000 births per year in WA (ABS). Approx 70–80% require GTT = ~13,000–14,000 tests/year in WA
- Perth metro share: ~80% = ~10,000–11,000 potential customers/year
- Addressable per week: ~200 Perth GTT tests/week

## Operating Model

### Pathology Partnership (Critical Path Item #1)
- Approach PathWest and Western Diagnostic Pathology (WDP) for satellite/mobile collection
- They provide phlebotomist + equipment; we provide the collection room
- Results turnaround: same session (~2 hrs)
- No NATA accreditation required by GTT Center Perth (pathology partner holds accreditation)
- Alternative: hire own phlebotomist + apply for Approved Collection Centre status (higher complexity, v2 option)

### Subtenant Model
- Self-employed practitioners pay weekly room/chair rent: est. A$150–300/week per position
- They set own prices, keep own revenue, hold own insurance (public liability required)
- Available minimum hours aligned with GTT session windows (9am–4pm weekdays)
- Operate under GTT Center Perth branding while on-site

### 3D Scan Partnership
- Approach existing Perth keepsake operators: Cedar 4D, Oh Hey Baby, Bloom Baby, 3D Baby Perth
- Offer them a permanent room in venue on revenue-share or fixed room rent
- They bring equipment, training, insurance — zero capex for GTT Center Perth

## Regulatory Checklist

- [ ] 3D keepsake ultrasound: not regulated by AHPRA. Legal as entertainment/photography service. No diagnostic claims permitted. ✓ Confirmed.
- [ ] Phlebotomy: no statutory registration for pathology collectors in Australia. Cert III HLT37215 minimum. ✓ Confirmed (partner model bypasses this entirely).
- [ ] Massage: registered with Association of Massage Therapists or equivalent. Public liability insurance required.
- [ ] Nails: no licensing in WA beyond general business/ABN. TAFE Certificate III in Nail Technology preferred.
- [ ] Hair: hairdressing licence (Certificate III in Hairdressing) required for employed staff. Subtenants self-responsible.
- [ ] Food/snacks (if offered): food handling certificate. Basic registration with local council.
- [ ] Venue: planning approval for health/beauty premises use. Confirm with City of Perth or relevant LGA.

## Competitive Moat

- **First mover in WA:** No equivalent exists in Perth
- **Captive audience:** Women are already committed to the 3-hour wait — this converts dead time, not discretionary time
- **Referral flywheel:** Every midwife who recommends = ongoing stream of captive bookings
- **Network effect:** Subtenants market to their own clients, who also become GTT Center Perth customers
