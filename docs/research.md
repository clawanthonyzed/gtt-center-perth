## 1. THE GTT PROCEDURE — EXACT DETAILS

### What It Is
The GTT (Glucose Tolerance Test) and the Gestational Diabetes Test are **the same test** in Australia. Official name: **75g Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT)**. It is the gold-standard universal screening method for Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM) in Australia, per ADIPS 2025 guidelines.

### Who Gets It
- Recommended for **all pregnant women** between **24–28 weeks** gestation
- Early testing (before 20 weeks) for women with prior GDM or HbA1c 6.0–6.4%
- 94% of Australian centres use universal screening at 24–28 weeks
- **~100% of pregnant women are offered the test**; approximately 18% test positive

### Step-by-Step Timing
| Time | Event |
|---|---|
| **T minus 8–12 hrs** | Fasting begins (overnight fast, water allowed) |
| **T=0 (Arrival)** | Fasting blood draw taken |
| **T=0 + 5 min** | Patient drinks **75g glucose dissolved in 250–300mL water** (must finish within 5 minutes) |
| **T=60 min** | Second blood draw |
| **T=120 min** | Third (final) blood draw |
| *T=180 (optional)* | Some protocols include a 3-hour draw |

**Total on-site time: 2 to 2.5 hours.** Patient **cannot leave between draws.**

### ADIPS 2025 Diagnostic Thresholds (Updated June 2025)
- Fasting: ≥5.3 mmol/L
- 1-hour: ≥10.6 mmol/L
- 2-hour: ≥9.0 mmol/L

### Critical Business Insight — Appointment Timing
- **GTT appointments MUST START before 10:00am** (PathWest confirmed; patients must fast overnight)
- Typical start window: **7:30am – 9:30am**
- Patient departure: **10:00am – 12:00pm**
- **Venue operates primarily 7:30am – 12:30pm for GTT bookings**
- Afternoon slots: available for standalone spa bookings, classes, subtenant own clients

## 3. PATHOLOGY OPTIONS — IN-HOUSE VS PARTNER

### Option A: Partner Model (Recommended for Launch)
PathWest or Western Diagnostic Pathology (WDP) operate a satellite/mobile collection point at the venue.

**What it requires from GTT Center Perth:**
- Compliant collection room (see spec below)
- Agreement with pathology provider

**What the pathology provider supplies:**
- Phlebotomist (their staff)
- Equipment and consumables
- Processing and results delivery (to referring doctor)
- They hold NATA accreditation and Medicare approval — venue does NOT need accreditation

**Revenue model:** Neutral cross-referral, or pathology pays small room hire fee. All Medicare billing handled by pathology provider. Patient pays pathology directly (bulk-billed in most cases).

### Option B: Own Approved Collection Centre (v2+)
**Steps required:**
1. NATA accreditation (ISO 15189) — up to **6 months** from application; **$2,000+ application fee** + ongoing NATA fees
2. Medicare approval as Approved Collection Centre — submit via PRODA/HPOS; max 3 months before trading
3. Hire phlebotomist with **Cert III HLT37215** (35+ hours supervised clinical practice required)
4. Comply with NSQHS Standards and ACSQHC Guidelines for Approved Pathology Collection Centres (3rd Edition 2013)

**Not recommended for v1** due to 6-month accreditation timeline and compliance overhead.

### Pathology Collection Room Specification (Both Options)
Minimum compliant room requirements:
- Individual patient bay/room with **privacy** (partitioned or separate room)
- **Wheelchair accessible** bay required
- **Phlebotomy chair** (ergonomic, supports patient safety)
- Sharps containers compliant with **AS/NZS 23907:2023** (puncture-resistant, leak-proof, biohazard labelled, elbow-height placement)
- Hand hygiene station (soap + ABHR — National Hand Hygiene Initiative compliant)
- 70% isopropyl alcohol wipes
- Latex-free tourniquets
- Vacuum blood collection tubes (colour-coded)
- Documentation and labelling per NPAAC guidelines
- Minimum floor area: **9 sqm** (practical minimum for compliant setup)

### 4.6 Spray Tan (Automated Booth — No Staff)

## 5. STAFFING MODELS

### Model Comparison

| Model | What It Is | Tax/Legal | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Room/chair rental (licence)** | Practitioner pays venue weekly rent; runs own business | Property licence — NOT employment, NOT contracting. GST applies to rent. | Lowest. Not sham contracting if structured correctly. |
| **Independent contractor** | Venue pays practitioner per job; venue controls scope | ATO "whole of relationship" test (from 26 Aug 2024). Must pass new test. | Medium. Risk if practitioner has no other clients or venue controls hours. |
| **Employee (casual)** | Venue employs; pays Award wages + super + casual loading | MA000005 applies. Casual conversion rights after 6 months of regular hours. | Highest obligation but most flexibility for house services. |

### Room Rental Model — Correct Structure
A **Room Licence Agreement** (not a subcontract for services):
- Practitioner holds own ABN, brand, pricing, booking system
- Venue does NOT control hours, prices, uniforms, or client systems
- Practitioner pays fixed weekly room/chair rent
- Practitioner supplies own equipment or pays documented consumables fee
- Venue charges **GST on room rental** (commercial taxable supply)
- No superannuation, PAYG, or leave obligations from venue

**Template cost:** A$200–500 from Lawpath or Sprintlaw; have commercial solicitor review before use.

### NEW 2024 LAW — Critical Alert
**Fair Work Legislation Amendment (Closing Loopholes No. 2) Act 2024 — effective 26 August 2024:**
Courts now look at **whole of relationship** (practical reality), not just the written contract. A person working exclusively for one venue, on a roster, following venue policies, in a uniform = likely employee regardless of what the contract says.

**Criminal penalties for sham contracting (from 1 January 2025):**
- Individual director: up to **2 years imprisonment + $469,500 fine**
- Business <15 employees: up to $99,000
- Business 15+ employees: up to $495,000 or 3x underpayment

FWO + ATO joint sham contracting crackdown active in health/beauty/wellness sector as of March 2026.

**Solution:** Use genuine room rental licence. Practitioners must have autonomy.

## 11. OPEN LOUNGE — PREMIUM DESIGN

The lounge is the centrepiece. Patients spend 2.5 hours here. This is where the MIWM model falls short (clinical focus). GTT Center Perth lounge should feel closer to a private hospital VIP suite or premium airport lounge.

### Essential Elements
- Premium cushioned seating (pregnancy-specific: wide, supportive, easy to get in/out of)
- Phone charging points at every seat
- Filtered water station + herbal teas + approved snacks
- Wi-Fi (fast, named: "GTT Center")
- Soft lighting (dimmable)
- Gentle background music or white noise (not a radio — curated playlist)

### Premium Add-Ons
- Heated throw blankets
- Eye masks (packaged, complimentary)
- Pregnancy reading material (not magazines — curated pregnancy books, birth prep cards)
- Small screen with optional birth prep/hypnobirthing short videos
- Partner/family area with comfortable seating separate from patient area

### What NOT to Serve in Lounge During GTT
- **NO food or caffeinated drinks while glucose is processing** (T=0 to T=120) — will invalidate results
- After T=120 (final draw): snacks can be offered
- Water and herbal non-caffeinated tea: safe throughout

### Food Business Registration (WA)
- Register as a **food business** with local council (e.g. City of Perth) under *Food Act 2008*
- Low-risk food businesses (packaged snacks, bottled water, herbal tea): **notification only** (simpler process)
- Must have at least one **Food Safety Supervisor** (nationally recognised training course)
- All food handlers complete basic food safety training
- Annual fee varies by council; typically A$100–300/year for low-risk notification
- Register BEFORE serving any food or drink

## 13. FOOD AND DRINKS

### Regulatory (WA — Food Act 2008)
- **Low-risk food business notification:** For venues serving pre-packaged foods, bottled water, herbal tea (no cooking, no raw meat, no high-risk foods)
- Register with local council environmental health team
- Annual notification fee: varies by council (~A$150–300)
- Food Safety Supervisor: 1 person must complete approved training (e.g. SITXFSA005 "Use Hygienic Practices for Food Safety")
- All staff handling food complete basic food handler training

### What to Serve
| Item | Timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Still water, sparkling water | All times | Always available |
| Herbal tea (non-caffeinated) | All times | Peppermint, chamomile, pregnancy blends |
| Packaged protein bars, nuts | After T=120 (final draw) | Pregnancy-safe brands only |
| Fresh fruit (pre-packaged) | After T=120 | Light, easy to eat |
| Electrolyte drinks | After T=120 | Coconut water, Hydralyte — recovery after fasting |

### What NOT to Serve
- Caffeinated drinks (coffee, black tea, energy drinks) — at any time during GTT
- High-sugar foods during test window
- Anything not individually packaged (food safety risk)
