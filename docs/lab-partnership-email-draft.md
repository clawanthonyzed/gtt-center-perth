## Why It's Written This Way (design notes, not for inclusion in the sent email)

- **No venture name.** "GTT Center Perth" is a working placeholder, not a final brand name (see `brand-guide.md`). Naming a placeholder in outreach to an external partner risks it sticking or being repeated back, and commits to nothing that hasn't been decided.
- **Planning/feasibility framing, not "opening."** Anthony's standing correction (also noted in `HANDOFF.md` and `docs/05_open_questions_for_founder.md` Q6) is to avoid "we are establishing/opening" language before a location or partnership is confirmed. This draft says "exploring options... currently in the planning/feasibility stage" throughout.
- **No GTT test duration/mechanics explanation.** The original draft over-explained what a GTT is and how long it takes to the pathology provider itself — this is unnecessary and slightly presumptuous; pathology labs run these tests as routine business and don't need it explained to them.
- **No phone call ask.** Anthony wants an email reply only. The draft explicitly asks for an email reply and explicitly says "I'd prefer to keep this to email for now rather than a call," rather than the previous version's "would you be available for a 20-minute call this week?"
- **Two specific questions included**, matching Anthony's actual follow-up intent once a reply arrives (see `cutoff-time-CORRECTION.md`): the specimen cutoff time, and whether overnight storage + next-day collection is viable as an alternative model. Asking both upfront (rather than cutoff-time-only, then a second follow-up email) saves a round trip.
