## SECTION 1 — ABOUT GTT CENTER PERTH AND THIS SERVICE

**GTT Center Perth** is a wellness venue that facilitates Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (GTT/OGTT) collection on behalf of licensed pathology providers. GTT Center Perth is not a pathology laboratory.

**What GTT Center Perth does:**
- Provides a licensed blood collection facility (NATA-accredited collection centre under [WDP/PathWest])
- Employs a qualified phlebotomist (Certificate III Pathology Collection HLT37215 minimum) to collect your blood samples
- Coordinates your appointment timing and wellness services during your waiting period

**What GTT Center Perth does NOT do:**
- We do NOT process or analyse your blood samples (this is done by [WDP/PathWest] laboratory)
- We do NOT receive or hold your test results
- We do NOT communicate your GTT results to you — results are sent by the laboratory to your referring doctor
- We do NOT make any clinical decisions about your health or your test

**For your GTT results: Contact your GP, obstetrician, or midwife after your appointment. Allow 1–2 business days for results to reach them.**

## SECTION 2 — THE PROCEDURE

The Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT) involves:

1. **Fasting blood draw (T=0):** A blood sample is collected from a vein in your arm after your overnight fast. A fine needle is inserted into a vein (usually inside the elbow). This takes approximately 5–10 minutes.

2. **Glucose drink:** You will be given a drink containing exactly 75 grams of glucose dissolved in water. You must drink this within 5 minutes.

3. **Waiting period (approximately 2 hours):** You must remain at the venue. You may not eat, drink (except water), or exercise during this time.

4. **1-hour blood draw (T=60):** A second blood sample is collected approximately 60 minutes after you finish the glucose drink.

5. **2-hour blood draw (T=120):** A third blood sample is collected approximately 120 minutes after you finish the glucose drink.

**Total time at venue: approximately 2.5 hours.**

Each blood draw involves a needle being inserted into your vein. There may be minor discomfort, bruising, or soreness at the collection site.

## SECTION 3 — POSSIBLE RISKS AND SIDE EFFECTS

**Common (may affect up to 1 in 3 patients):**
- Nausea after drinking the glucose solution
- Mild dizziness or light-headedness
- Discomfort at the venepuncture site
- Minor bruising at the collection site

**Less common (may affect up to 1 in 20 patients):**
- Vomiting after the glucose drink — if this occurs, the test cannot be completed that day
- Fainting or near-fainting (vasovagal reaction) — our staff are trained to respond

**Rare:**
- Infection at the venepuncture site
- Haematoma (significant bruising/swelling)

**If you have a reaction:** Our staff will assist you immediately. If a medical emergency occurs, we will call 000. You should inform your referring doctor of any adverse reactions.

---

## SECTION 4 — YOUR RESPONSIBILITIES

Before your appointment, you must:
- Fast for at least 8 hours (no food, no beverages except water, no cigarettes)
- Bring your pathology request form
- Bring your Medicare card
- Not exercise on the morning of your test

During your appointment, you must:
- Remain at the venue for the full 2.5 hours
- Not eat, drink (except water), or exercise between the glucose drink and your final blood draw
- Inform staff immediately if you feel unwell
