## Summary

GTT Center Perth will operate under **Option A: WDP Licensed Collection Centre (LCC)** arrangement. This is the only model being pursued. Option B (own Approved Pathology Authority) has been ruled out — it requires 12–18 months of NATA accreditation and is not compatible with an October 2026 launch.

Under Option A:
- GTT Center Perth provides: venue, collection room, employed phlebotomists, equipment
- WDP provides: accreditation umbrella, consumables (tubes, glucose drink, requisition forms), specimen transport, laboratory analysis, Medicare billing, result reporting to GP
- **GTT Center Perth earns zero from pathology billing.** All revenue comes from packages and services.

## 3. The Revenue Model — Zero Pathology Income

GTT Center Perth earns **nothing** from the pathology service. This must be understood clearly:

| What happens | Who benefits |
|-------------|-------------|
| GTT client has blood collected | Patient gets required diagnostic test |
| WDP analyses samples at their Myaree lab | WDP earns the Medicare fee |
| WDP reports results to referring GP | GP and patient get clinical outcome |
| GTT Center Perth provides the space and experience | GTT Center Perth earns **from packages only** |

**This is the model.** GTT Center Perth's value proposition to WDP: we bring them a captive stream of patients (176+/month) who they would otherwise never capture. WDP's value to us: clinical legitimacy, accreditation umbrella, no Medicare billing complexity.

## 4. Collection Room — Regulatory Framework

GTT Center Perth's collection room must meet ACSQHC *Guidelines for Approved Pathology Collection Centres* (3rd Edition, 2013). WDP will inspect the room before operating from our site.

### Minimum Room Specifications

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|---------|
| Floor area | Minimum 9 sqm (we are targeting 20 sqm with 2 chairs) |
| Floor finish | Non-slip seamless vinyl or tile — NO carpet. Coved skirting. |
| Walls | Washable, moisture-resistant, light-coloured (semi-gloss minimum) |
| Lighting | Minimum 400 lux at work surface, CRI >85 LED |
| Ventilation | Minimum 6 ACH (air changes per hour). Separate exhaust strongly recommended — NOT shared recirculating HVAC |
| Hand hygiene | **Mandatory at room entry:** elbow-operated or sensor taps (not standard taps), liquid soap dispenser, paper towel dispenser, ABHR (alcohol hand rub) — all wall-mounted |
| Power | Minimum 4 GPOs at bench height (centrifuge, label printer, chair lighting, device) |
| Door | Minimum 850mm clear width (DDA wheelchair/pram access) |
| Privacy | Solid door (not curtain). Soundproofing preferred. |
| Location | Adjacent to lounge/waiting area — NOT accessible only through treatment rooms |

### Essential Equipment (GTT Collection Room)

| Item | Specification | Cost Estimate | Qty |
|------|--------------|--------------|-----|
| Phlebotomy chair | Reclining to flat, vinyl/wipe-clean, bariatric-capable | A$600–1,500 | 2 |
| Tabletop centrifuge | Min 3,000 RPM, refrigerated preferred. Hettich EBA 200 (A$1,800) or Universal 320R (A$3,500) | A$1,800–5,000 | 1 |
| Specimen fridge (medical-grade) | 4°C with temperature logging (not a bar fridge) | A$800–1,500 | 1 |
| Sharps containers | AS/NZS 23907:2023 compliant, wall-mounted brackets at elbow height | A$50–100 | 4 |
| Biohazard waste bins | Yellow labelled, lidded | A$70–120 | 2 |
| Blood spill kit | TGA-listed disinfectant + absorbent + PPE | A$160–240 | 2 |
| Thermal label printer | Zebra ZD420 or equivalent | A$350 | 1 |
| Consumables cabinet | Lockable, wall-mounted or under-bench | A$200–400 | 1 |
| Emergency call button | Connected to reception (patient vasovagal/collapse alert) | A$200–500 | 1 |

### Critical: The Centrifuge Rule

**GDM is systematically underdiagnosed at collection centres that do not centrifuge within 10 minutes.** The WA ORCHID study (PMC6888052) found that failure to centrifuge NaF/fluoride-oxalate tubes within 10 minutes causes in-vitro glycolysis that **misses 62% of GDM diagnoses.**

GTT Center Perth will centrifuge on-site immediately after each draw. This is our clinical quality differentiator vs standard pathology waiting rooms. WDP will confirm if they require us to centrifuge or if they prefer to receive unspun samples — get this in writing.

## 5. Phlebotomist Staffing

GTT Center Perth employs **2 phlebotomists from Day 1** (Chair A / Chair B). The model:

- **Qualification:** Certificate III or IV Pathology (HLT37215 / HLT47015) or Certificate IV in Nursing with phlebotomy training. Not AHPRA-registered.
- **Award:** Health Professionals Award MA000027, Pathology Collector classification, ~A$24.50/hr base, 25hr/week AM shift = A$43,068/yr including super.
- **Operating under:** WDP's Licensed Collection Centre accreditation — phlebotomists work within WDP's quality management system (QMS) and comply with their standard operating procedures
- **Training:** WDP may require our phlebotomists to complete their specific SOP training — confirm in partnership negotiation

## 6. ADIPS 2025 Thresholds — What Our Phlebotomists Must Know

ADIPS updated GDM diagnostic thresholds in 2025. All GTT Center Perth clinical materials use these values:

| Measurement | ADIPS 2025 Threshold |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Fasting plasma glucose | ≥ 5.3 mmol/L |
| 1-hour plasma glucose | ≥ 10.6 mmol/L |
| 2-hour plasma glucose | ≥ 9.0 mmol/L |

If any single value meets or exceeds the threshold → GDM positive → result reported to referring GP by WDP.

**GTT Center Perth never communicates results to patients. Results go WDP → GP → patient.**

## 7. Option B — Reference Only (Not Pursuing)

Becoming an Approved Pathology Authority (own ACC, Medicare-billing) requires:
- NATA accreditation (ISO 15189): 4–6 months, A$4,500–7,500
- Approved Pathology Authority registration
- NATA-accredited Clinical Scientist on staff
- Timeline: 12–18 months minimum

Option B has been ruled out — not compatible with October 2026 launch. The WDP Option A LCC model is the permanent strategy (per Day 49 decision, confirmed in `option-b-collection-centre.md`).
