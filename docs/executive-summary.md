## What This Business Is

GTT Center Perth is a concept for Western Australia's first premium wellness venue built around the Glucose Tolerance Test (GTT) — the mandatory gestational diabetes screening test that pregnant women undergo, typically around 24-28 weeks gestation.

Instead of a bare pathology waiting room while fasting, patients would spend the ~2-2.5 hour test window receiving massage, nails, brows, or hairdressing, bundled into one of two fixed-price packages with venue/lounge access included free ([services-pricing-locked.md](services-pricing-locked.md)).

**Entity:** YETI Holding Trust, trading as GTT Center Perth
**Corporate trustee:** YETI Tipi Holdings PTY LTD
**Founders:** Anthony Zed (trustee, non-operational — administrative oversight only) + Imara (funding partner)
**Funding:** Self-funded via Anthony's partner's savings (~A$200,000), no external investor sought to date
**Status:** Pre-launch, planning/feasibility stage. **No launch date is set** — the venture proceeds by dependency sequencing, not a calendar target (standing instruction; see `docs/04_roadmap_next_steps.md`).

## The Problem

Pregnant women undergoing a GTT typically fast for 8+ hours, then attend a pathology collection point for a series of blood draws spread across roughly 2-2.5 hours, with limited guidance on how to spend the intervening wait. No dedicated wellness-integrated GTT venue was identified operating in WA at the time this venture's market research was conducted ([market-research-findings.md](market-research-findings.md)) — this is a research finding from this venture's own investigation, not an independently audited market claim.

---

## The Solution

A purpose-built venue where GTT blood draws happen on-site (phlebotomists employed directly by GTT Center Perth, operating under a partner pathology provider's NATA accreditation — see Pathology Model below), and the wait between draws is filled with wellness services that are never interrupted by a blood draw ([services-pricing-locked.md](services-pricing-locked.md) Part A, [operations-manual.md](operations-manual.md)).

**The only comparable business identified in research is MIWM in Melbourne** ([market-research-findings.md](market-research-findings.md)) — reported fully booked 3-4 weeks in advance, single-client-at-a-time model, no hairdressing. GTT Center Perth's current model is a multi-client concurrent design (10 simultaneous GTT clients under the current Scenario C plan) plus a full hairdressing menu.

---

## Pathology Model — Option A (Confirmed Approach)

GTT Center Perth partners with an already-NATA-accredited pathology provider for collection oversight, rather than building an in-house accredited lab. **WDP (Western Diagnostic Pathology) is the priority-1 partner; PathWest and Clinipath are contingency options if WDP declines or terms are unfavourable** ([reed-partnerships.md](reed-partnerships.md)).

- **GTT Center Perth provides:** the venue, a compliant collection room, and 2 employed phlebotomists
- **The pathology partner provides:** the NATA accreditation umbrella, specimen processing, and result reporting to the referring doctor
- **GTT Center Perth does not:** diagnose, interpret, or communicate test results

**Building an in-house NATA/RCPA-accredited collection centre (Option B) was investigated and rejected** — see [option-b-collection-centre.md](option-b-collection-centre.md) and [inhouse-gtt-research.md](inhouse-gtt-research.md) — primarily due to the 12-18 month accreditation timeline.

**Partnership status (2026-07-19):** WDP has been emailed, awaiting reply. PathWest and Clinipath have not yet been contacted (1 of 3 potential partners approached so far).

## Current Operating Model

Per [HANDOFF.md](HANDOFF.md) (2026-07-17, the most recent session summary and source of truth for the active plan):

- **AM GTT:** 10 clients/day, 07:00 start, 2 chairs, 2 phlebotomists, synchronized-start schedule (Scenario C — `docs/scenario-c-sync-timetables.md`, fully verified with zero double-bookings)
- **Packages:** 2 tiers only, renamed/renumbered 2026-07-20 — Package 1 (A$250, fixed 2×30min) and Package 2 (A$300, flexible composition), same 2 price points as before, not a re-opening of the earlier decision to drop the old A$200 tier ([services-pricing-locked.md](services-pricing-locked.md))
- **AM downtime fill:** the 8 AM treatment staff (massage/nail/hair/beauty) are not fully occupied for the entire GTT window — each client's own service only fills part of their ~2-2.5hr visit. The model is built so this same AM staff also takes standalone, non-GTT bookings during the gaps between their rostered GTT clients' services, not just a dedicated separate shift.
- **PM shift:** Individual standalone services (not packages), 4 dedicated casual hires (1 each: massage, hair, nail, beauty), cross-shift qualified with AM staff, costed on actual hours worked
- **Saturday:** Reuses the AM Scenario C model + PM standalone, costed as a bolt-on shift
- **Sunday:** Closed until standalone PM service demand is proven and profitable to justify the additional penalty-rate staffing cost — a conditional business choice, not a fixed-forever position, and not a legal requirement (the WA Retail Trading Hours Act exempts this business type from trading-hour restriction)

**Known documentation gap:** [operations-manual.md](operations-manual.md)'s own detailed scheduling section still describes the earlier, superseded 8-client Scenario B model rather than the current 10-client Scenario C model — flagged and not yet fully rewritten, see `docs/01_conflicts_log.md` CONFLICT-03.

**Staffing:** 12 employed roles (2 phlebotomists, 8 AM treatment staff across 4 service categories, 1 receptionist/manager, plus the 4-person PM roster) plus a Venue Manager (Managing Director) as the critical-path first hire. **Recruitment does not begin until a physical venue location is confirmed** (direct founder instruction, 2026-07-19) — see `docs/venue-manager-job-posting.md` (built and ready, not yet posted).

**Why break-even is shown this way (corrected 2026-07-20):** a single blended "visits/month" figure treats a GTT-package sale (~A$250-300) and a PM standalone service (~A$95) as interchangeable units, which understates how different they actually are — this is also why this venture now tracks **packages/services sold**, not "client visits," as its core revenue metric (a visit alone carries no dollar figure). The real model blends roughly 220 AM GTT packages sold/month (10/day × 22 days) with roughly 350 PM standalone services/month, together covering the ~A$88,625/month cost base with margin to spare (the source of the ~A$25,087/month profit figure above). The two single-segment bookends in the table exist only to make the per-sale-type spend difference concrete, not as a realistic standalone scenario.

Correcting the AM revenue to the current 10-client Scenario C figure resolves the discrepancy: [profit-loss-tables.md](profit-loss-tables.md) v2.0's whole-venture profitability figure (+A$25,087.07/month, matching [HANDOFF.md](HANDOFF.md)) is the confirmed current authoritative figure.
