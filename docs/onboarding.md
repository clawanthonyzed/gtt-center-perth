## A2. Day 1 Orientation (Operations Director leads — 2 hours)

### Tour (30 min)
- Reception: Fresha terminal, phone, patient files location, welcome pack storage
- Lounge: snack station, seating layout, patient flow through lounge
- Treatment rooms: linen location, product storage, equipment setup
- Collection room: phlebotomist workspace (do not enter without need)
- Staff room: lockers, break area
- Emergency exits, fire extinguisher locations
- First aid kit location

### Mission Briefing (15 min)
"GTT Center Perth exists because pregnant women spend 3 hours in pathology waiting rooms with nothing to do. We're the first business in WA built for this. Every person who walks through this door is fasting, probably anxious, and carrying a baby. Our job is to make this the best morning of their pregnancy."

### Fresha Walkthrough (30 min — receptionist only; others: their own booking view only)
- How bookings appear
- Patient notes field (add any relevant preferences)
- How to check in a patient
- How to process payment
- How to add a booking note
- How to see tomorrow's schedule

### Service Standards Briefing (15 min)
- Patient is always addressed by first name
- No discussing GTT results (ever) — redirect to their doctor
- Fasting failure: do not dismiss, notify Operations Director immediately
- Any patient who feels unwell: alert phlebotomist + Operations Director immediately
- Phone use: personal phones off during treatment delivery

### Buddy System (Weeks 1–2)
- New staff shadow Operations Director or an experienced team member for first 3 shifts
- No solo patient contact until buddy sign-off (Operations Director confirms readiness)

## A3. Week 1 Training Schedule

### All Staff (Days 1–3)
- Day 1: Orientation (as above)
- Day 2: Observe full operating day (shadowing)
- Day 3: Manage own patient interactions with Operations Director oversight

### Phlebotomist Only
- WDP/PathWest phlebotomy protocol review (WDP-provided)
- GTT clinical protocol (gtt-clinical-protocol.md) — read and sign
- Adverse event protocol — read, sign, verbal quiz by Imara
- First collection: Imara present in building

### Massage Therapist Only
- Review GTT Center Perth pregnancy massage intake form
- Contraindications review: conditions requiring GP clearance before treatment
  - Pre-eclampsia symptoms, placenta praevia, very active morning sickness, multiple gestation with complications
- Booking note review: how to access patient's trimester info before appointment

### Nail Tech / Beauty Therapist Only
- Product list review: approved products only (pregnancy-safe)
- LEV (local exhaust ventilation) operation: nail station ventilation must be running at all times
- Waste disposal: chemical waste (polish remover, peroxide) — correct bin

## A4. 30-Day Review (Imara conducts)

Topics:
1. How is the role going? Any blockers?
2. What feedback have you received from patients?
3. Any supply or equipment issues?
4. Performance against standards (punctuality, patient interactions, cleanliness)
5. Any training needs identified?
6. Confirm: are all certifications/memberships still current?

Outcome: either confirm ongoing employment, identify improvement areas (with 30-day plan), or in rare cases escalate to performance management.

# PART B — CLIENT ONBOARDING

## B1. Before Booking (Awareness → Decision)

**How they find us:**
- Referral card from midwife or OB clinic
- Instagram (organic or paid)
- Google search ("GTT test Perth")
- Word of mouth from another patient
- Pathology partner cross-referral (if WDP includes us in patient communication)

**Booking funnel:**
1. Lands on website → sees "Book your GTT experience"
2. Selects GTT date/time slot
3. Selects services (limited to what fits in their GTT window)
4. Enters details: name, mobile, email, due date, referral form availability
5. Pays A$30 deposit
6. Receives instant confirmation email + calendar invite (.ics)

## B6. Arrival Experience (First 5 Minutes)

This is the most critical touchpoint. Patient is fasting, anxious, possibly first GTT.

**Receptionist script:**
> "Good morning [Name]! Welcome — we've been expecting you. I'm [Receptionist Name]. Have you had anything to eat or drink this morning besides water? Great. Can I quickly grab your referral form and Medicare card? Lovely. Let me take you through to [Phlebotomist Name] for your first blood draw, and then the rest of the morning is yours."

**Do NOT say:**
- "Take a seat" (they've been waiting in pathology rooms before — we're different)
- "Fill in this form" (welcome pack is pre-printed, they just confirm details)
- Anything that implies this will be like a normal pathology visit

**Immediate comfort offer:**
- "Would you like a glass of water while [Phlebotomist] gets set up?"
- After T=0 draw: "Now the first part's done — what would you like to drink? We have [herbal tea menu], still water, sparkling..."

## B7. During the Visit

Receptionist tracks patient's schedule throughout the morning:
- T=60 draw: escort from service room 5 min before draw time ("Just popping back to [Phlebotomist] for your one-hour blood draw — quick and painless!")
- T=120 draw: same ("Last one — you're almost done!")
- Post T=120: snacks delivered to patient immediately, receptionist thanks them

## B9. Returning Customer Protocol

If patient books a second service visit (outside GTT — e.g., just massage or nails):
- Fresha flags as returning customer
- Receptionist notes this in their Fresha profile
- Greet with recognition: "Welcome back, [Name]! How's [baby]?"
- No fasting instructions (not a GTT visit)
- Same quality standards apply

## B10. Referral Request Protocol

After every visit, patient receives:
1. Verbal from receptionist: "If you have a friend who's also doing their GTT soon, we'd love a referral — we're still brand new and every recommendation helps."
2. Referral cards left in patient handbag at end of visit (2 cards)
3. Post-visit email includes referral mention
4. Future: referral reward program (discount or credit for next visit) — implement at Month 3

## B11. Complaint Handling

**Step 1:** Receptionist receives complaint (verbal or written)
- Acknowledge immediately: "I'm so sorry to hear that. I want to make sure this is sorted for you."
- Do not argue, dismiss, or over-explain
- Escalate to Imara within 30 minutes (same day)

**Step 2:** Imara calls patient within 4 hours
- Listen without interruption
- Apologise genuinely for their experience
- Ask what would make it right

**Step 3:** Resolution options (Imara decides)
- Partial or full refund
- Complimentary service on next visit
- Explanation (if clinical: factual, not defensive)

**Step 4:** Document in complaints register with outcome
- Keep for 7 years
- If complaint involves clinical element: notify WDP/PathWest and solicitor

**Response time SLA:**
- Same-day acknowledgement: 100%
- Resolution (or resolution plan communicated): within 48 hours
