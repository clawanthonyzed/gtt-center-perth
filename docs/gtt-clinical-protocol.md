## 1. IN-HOUSE GTT MODEL — STRUCTURE

GTT Center Perth employs its own phlebotomist(s) and manages the full GTT appointment experience. Pathology processing and result reporting are handled by the partner laboratory.

```
GTT Center Perth (venue + phlebotomist employer)
        │
        ├── Employs: Phlebotomist (Cert III HLT37215+)
        ├── Provides: ACSQHC-compliant collection room
        ├── Manages: Patient scheduling and experience
        │
        └── Partners with: Western Diagnostic Pathology (WDP) [PRIORITY]
                │   OR PathWest (secondary)
                │
                └── WDP/PathWest provides:
                        ├── NATA accreditation umbrella (Licensed Collection Centre)
                        ├── Specimen processing and analysis
                        ├── Result reporting to referring doctor/midwife
                        └── Phlebotomist credentialing/registration as approved collector
```

**Critical:** GTT Center Perth's phlebotomist must be registered as an approved collector under WDP's NATA accreditation BEFORE performing any collections. This is established during the pathology partnership setup (see reed-partnerships.md).

## 2. GTT PROCEDURE — CLINICAL OVERVIEW

### What is the GTT?
The Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT / GTT) screens for Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM).

**Indication:** Offered to all pregnant women at 24–28 weeks gestation (Australian standard).  
**Standard:** ADIPS 2014 Consensus Guidelines / FIGO 2015 (75g glucose, 2-hour test).  
**Note:** GTT Center Perth does not make clinical judgements. All clinical decisions are made by the referring doctor or midwife. GTT Center Perth facilitates the collection only.

### GTT Timeline

```
T=0 (Arrival — 8:00am example)
  │   Patient arrives fasting (8+ hours, water only)
  │   Receptionist confirms fasting status, collects referral form + Medicare card
  │   Phlebotomist performs FIRST blood draw (fasting glucose)
  │   Patient drinks 75g glucose solution over 5 minutes
  │
T=0+5min (8:05am)
  │   Patient escorted to lounge / treatment room for services
  │   (Massage, nails, brows, scan — booked during this window)
  │
T=60 (9:00am)
  │   Phlebotomist performs SECOND blood draw (1-hour glucose)
  │   Patient returns to lounge or service continues if scheduled post-draw
  │
T=120 (10:00am)
  │   Phlebotomist performs THIRD blood draw (2-hour glucose)
  │   Patient receives snacks (herbal tea, coconut water, protein bar, fruit)
  │   Patient may leave after T=120 draw is complete
  │
T=120+30 (10:30am)
  │   Specimens dispatched to WDP/PathWest lab via courier or drop-off
  │   Results typically available same day or next business day
  │   Results sent by lab directly to referring doctor/midwife
```

### GTT Start Times (Staggered)

| Slot | Fasting draw | 1-hour draw | 2-hour draw | Latest depart |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Slot A | 7:30am | 8:30am | 9:30am | 9:45am |
| Slot B | 8:00am | 9:00am | 10:00am | 10:15am |
| Slot C | 8:30am | 9:30am | 10:30am | 10:45am |
| Slot D | 9:00am | 10:00am | 11:00am | 11:15am |

Maximum 4 concurrent GTT patients per day at launch (one phlebotomist managing staggered draws). Expand to 8 slots per day when second phlebotomist hired.

## 3. PATIENT PRE-ARRIVAL INSTRUCTIONS

Communicated via:
- Fresha confirmation email (immediate on booking)
- 48-hour SMS reminder (full fasting instructions)
- 24-hour SMS reminder (with fasting cutoff time personalised)
- 1-hour day-of SMS (with address and arrival instructions)

### Fasting Requirements (patient-facing language)

> **From midnight the night before your appointment:**
> - NO food or drinks except plain water
> - NO coffee, tea, juice, soft drink, or flavoured water
> - NO chewing gum, mints, or lollies
> - NO cigarettes (nicotine affects results)
> - NO exercise on the morning of your test
>
> **Water:** Drink plenty of water. Stay hydrated — it makes blood draws easier.
>
> **Bring:**
> - Your GP or midwife's pathology referral form (paper or eReferral)
> - Medicare card
> - Comfortable, loose clothing (we'll be drawing blood from your arm three times)
>
> **If you feel unwell during the test:** Tell our team immediately. Your safety comes first.

## 4. DAY-OF COLLECTION PROCEDURE

### Pre-Arrival (Phlebotomist, 7:15am)
- [ ] Check Fresha for day's bookings — confirm all patients have referral forms
- [ ] Inspect collection room: sharps disposal (Daniels Healthcare or equivalent), hand hygiene station stocked, biohazard waste bags in place
- [ ] Prepare collection supplies per patient: vacutainers (grey top/fluoride-oxalate for glucose), butterfly or straight needle, alcohol wipes, cotton, bandage
- [ ] Prepare 75g glucose solution (commercially prepared — Polycal or equivalent, NOT home-made): one bottle per patient, refrigerated, room temp 20 minutes before
- [ ] Confirm courier/lab dispatch time for specimens (coordinate with WDP driver schedule)
- [ ] Test glucose drink: confirm it is not expired

### On Patient Arrival
- [ ] Receptionist greets patient by name, confirms fasting ("When did you last eat/drink?")
- [ ] Receptionist collects referral form and Medicare card, scans/copies for record
- [ ] Receptionist escorts to collection room and introduces to phlebotomist
- [ ] Phlebotomist verifies: patient name, DOB, gestational age on referral
- [ ] Phlebotomist notes patient's arm preference, checks for veins (hydration, comfort)

### T=0 Fasting Draw
- [ ] Position patient (seated or reclining if preferred — pregnancy comfort)
- [ ] Draw fasting blood sample: grey top vacutainer (fluoride-oxalate), label immediately with patient name, DOB, time of draw, collector ID
- [ ] Record time on collection log
- [ ] Administer 75g glucose drink — instruct patient to consume within 5 minutes
- [ ] Note exact glucose drink finish time (this is T=0 for clinical purposes)
- [ ] Escort patient to lounge or treatment room

### T=60 Draw
- [ ] Alert via Fresha/phone 10 minutes before draw time
- [ ] Receptionist collects patient from service room if in treatment
- [ ] Draw 1-hour sample: label with patient name, DOB, time, collector ID
- [ ] Record on collection log
- [ ] Patient may return to service/lounge

### T=120 Draw (Final)
- [ ] Same procedure as T=60
- [ ] After draw: offer patient snacks (herbal tea, coconut water, protein bar) — MANDATORY
- [ ] Patient may leave only AFTER T=120 draw is complete
- [ ] Remind patient: "Your results will go directly to your doctor/midwife within 24–48 hours. Please follow up with them."
- [ ] GTT Center Perth does NOT communicate results to patients

### Post-Collection Specimen Handling
- [ ] All three vacutainers labelled, placed in biohazard bag with completed pathology request form
- [ ] Cool transport: specimens in cool pack for courier
- [ ] Complete WDP/PathWest specimen dispatch log
- [ ] Dispatch to lab at agreed time (latest: 11:30am daily — confirm with WDP)
- [ ] Retain copy of dispatch log for 7 years (patient record requirement)

## 5. COLLECTION ROOM REQUIREMENTS (ACSQHC Compliance)

**Room size:** Minimum 10 sqm (10–12 sqm recommended)  
**Layout:**
- Patient chair: adjustable, with armrest support
- Phlebotomist workspace: bench, good lighting (natural or 4000K+)
- Hand hygiene station: soap, paper towel, ABHR (alcohol-based hand rub)
- Sharps disposal: approved sharps container (Daniels Healthcare UltiMed or equivalent)
- Biohazard waste bin (yellow lid)
- Specimen refrigerator (2–8°C) with temperature log
- Privacy: door that closes, no window into room from public
- Wheelchair accessible (minimum 900mm door clearance)
- Emergency call button or intercom to reception

**Required signage (AS/NZS):**
- Biohazard symbol on room door
- Hand hygiene reminder at sink
- No entry when in use

**Compliance checks:**
- WorkSafe WA: general workplace safety
- WDP/PathWest site inspection before first collection (part of licensing agreement)
- Local council: confirm health use permits cover pathology collection (not just beauty)

## 6. ADVERSE EVENTS PROTOCOL

### Patient Feels Unwell During Test (vasovagal, fainting, nausea)

1. Keep patient seated or move to lying position immediately
2. Do NOT allow patient to stand if dizzy
3. Call Imara immediately
4. If unconscious or not responding: call 000 immediately
5. Do NOT administer food/glucose until after T=0 draw
6. If patient cannot complete the test (e.g., vomits the glucose drink): do not reattempt, notify referring doctor, reschedule
7. Document incident in incident register (date, time, what happened, action taken, outcome)

### Difficult Venepuncture
- Maximum 2 attempts per arm per phlebotomist
- If unsuccessful after 2 attempts: call WDP phlebotomy supervisor for guidance
- Never attempt more than 3 total attempts without senior clinical input
- Document all attempts in collection log

### Glucose Drink Refusal or Intolerance
- Cannot substitute the 75g glucose drink with food (food-based OGTT is not equivalent for GDM diagnosis)
- If patient refuses: document, notify referring doctor
- Do NOT pressure patient — their decision

### Anaphylaxis
- Phlebotomist must hold current anaphylaxis training
- Venue must hold EpiPen (Imara first aider)
- Refer to venue emergency management plan

## 7. RECORDS AND COMPLIANCE

### What we retain (7 years minimum)
- Collection log: patient name, DOB, gestational age, draw times, collector ID, glucose drink time
- Specimen dispatch log
- Adverse event register
- Equipment temperature logs (fridge)
- Pathology referral form copies

### What we do NOT retain
- GTT results (results go to lab → referring doctor → patient)
- Patient medical history beyond what's on the referral form
- GTT Center Perth does not store clinical results

### Privacy Act compliance
- Patient data stored on Fresha (booking details only) + paper records (collection log)
- Paper records: locked cabinet, access restricted to Imara and phlebotomist
- No patient data shared with third parties except the pathology partner (covered by referral form consent)

## 9. QUALITY ASSURANCE

### Daily
- Phlebotomist: collection log complete, specimens dispatched on time, fridge temp logged
- Imara: check adverse event register (no entries = good)

### Weekly
- Imara: review collection log for any anomalies
- Any WDP/PathWest feedback on specimen quality reviewed
- Near-miss register reviewed

### Monthly
- Imara + Cora (Clinical Coordinator agent): full clinical compliance review
- Check: all collection logs complete, all staff credentials current, supply inventory

### Annually
- WDP/PathWest site audit (part of NATA-accredited collection centre requirements)
- Phlebotomist CPR renewal check
- Update this protocol for any changes to ADIPS guidelines or WDP requirements
