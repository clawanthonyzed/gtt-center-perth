## 1. Current AM Max: 8 Clients/Day — Verified

Confirmed against the verified Scenario B timetable (`operations-manual.md`): 2 chairs, 2 phlebotomists, 20-minute staggered arrivals. Each client's full journey (first draw to departure) takes ~145 minutes. The AM window (07:30–12:30, 300 min) allows a last start of ~10:05, giving a usable 145-minute start window. At a 20-minute stagger, that supports 8 client starts — matches the current verified model exactly.

## 2. Growth Levers — What Increases AM Capacity

| Lever | Mechanism | Est. New Capacity | Risk/Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tighten stagger interval** (20min → 15min) | More client starts fit in the same window | ~10–11/day | Reduces phlebotomist buffer between draws from ~15–20min to ~10–15min for centrifuge/labelling/prep — tighter clinical margin, needs safety review |
| **Extend AM window** (start 07:00 or finish 13:00) | Directly adds start-window minutes | +1 client per ~20min added | Simple, but affects staff shift-start costs and client fasting-window messaging |
| **Add a 3rd phlebotomist/chair** | 3 chairs share the load — lets you tighten stagger to increase throughput WITHOUT tightening any individual chair's interval | ~10–12/day | Cleanest, safest growth path — recommended primary lever |

### Recommended: 3rd Chair + Modest Stagger Tightening

Adding a 3rd phlebotomist/chair and tightening stagger to 15 minutes (spread across 3 chairs instead of 2) keeps each individual chair's interval at ~45 minutes — **more comfortable than today's 40-minute interval**, while total daily throughput rises to an estimated **10–11 clients/day**. This is the path that reaches the previously-deferred "Scenario A, 10-client" target referenced elsewhere in the docs — it's now achievable with a concrete mechanism, not just a placeholder number.

## 3. Weekend Trading (Saturday + Sunday)

Confirmed legally permitted (personal services/health businesses are exempt from WA Retail Trading Hours Act restrictions — see prior session). This section treats weekend trading as a business decision already made, not a legal question.

**Model:** weekend days run as PM-style trading — standalone individual services + packages (once the PM package pricing question is resolved) — no AM GTT testing on weekends (assumed, since GP/midwife referral coordination and fasting logistics are weekday-oriented; confirm this assumption with Anthony before finalising).
