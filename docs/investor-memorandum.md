## Executive Summary

**GTT Center Perth is WA's first dedicated Glucose Tolerance Test (GTT) wellness centre** — a purpose-built premium experience for pregnant women undergoing the mandatory 75g Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT) for gestational diabetes screening.

Every pregnant woman in Australia is offered this test between 24–28 weeks gestation. The procedure requires a 2–2.5 hour on-site wait during which the patient cannot leave. Currently, this wait happens in standard pathology waiting rooms.

**GTT Center Perth transforms that wait into a premium day spa visit**: pregnancy massage, facial, gel nails, blowdry, lash lift, spray tan, and a full beauty service menu — all booked as a Package alongside the blood collection appointment. Blood collection is performed on-site by employed phlebotomists under a WDP/PathWest licensed collection centre arrangement.

**The only comparable service in Australia is MIWM in Melbourne** — single-client model, GP-led, Victoria only, 3–4 week wait times. GTT Center Perth runs 8 concurrent clients in the morning GTT window and adds a full afternoon session (PM Spa Packages) for non-GTT clients, generating a second revenue stream from the same premises.

## 1. The Market Opportunity

### The GTT — Universal Screening, Mandatory Wait

The 75g Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT) is the gold-standard screening method for Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM) in Australia, mandated by ADIPS 2025 guidelines and recommended for **100% of pregnant women** between 24–28 weeks gestation.

| Statistic | Figure | Source |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Australian births per year | ~277,000 | ABS 2024 |
| GTT tests performed nationally per year | ~270,000 | AIHW estimate |
| WA births per year | ~18,000 | ABS |
| Perth metro births per year | ~14,400 | ~80% metro share |
| **Perth GTT tests per year** | **~14,400** | Universal screening rate |
| **Perth GTT tests per week** | **~277** | 14,400 ÷ 52 |
| GDM diagnosis rate | 18% | AIHW 2023–24 |
| Perth GDM diagnoses per year | ~2,600 | 18% of 14,400 |

**GTT Center Perth's initial capacity is 176 GTT visits/month (8/day × 22 days)** — approximately 15% of Perth's total weekly GTT volume. A conservative first-year capture target for a single venue.

### The Unmet Need

The current patient experience is universally poor:
- Patients sit in standard pathology waiting rooms for 2–2.5 hours
- They are fasting, frequently anxious, sometimes nauseous
- There is no comfort or service offering anywhere in WA
- The only national competitor (MIWM Melbourne) operates a single-client model with no WA presence

**The WA market is uncontested.**

### The Referral Engine — Organic and Low-Cost

| Source | Mechanism |
|--------|-----------|
| Referring OB/GPs | Partner program — GP receives booking link, includes in GTT referral letter |
| Midwives (KEMH, JHC, FSH) | Warm referral at antenatal appointment |
| Instagram / social media | Pregnancy community word-of-mouth (see MIWM: 3–4 week wait with no paid marketing) |
| WDP/PathWest | Co-marketing via pathology partner booking infrastructure |

MIWM Melbourne operates on **zero paid advertising** and maintains 3–4 week wait times. Demand in this category is structural — driven by clinical mandation, not marketing.

## 2. The Business Model

### Service Packages — What Clients Buy

Every visit (AM GTT or PM Spa Package) is sold as one of three Packages:

| Package | Inclusions | Price |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| **Package 1** | Venue + lounge access (free) + any 2 × 30-min services | **A$200** |
| **Package 2** | Venue + lounge access (free) + 1 × 30-min + 1 × 45-min service | **A$250** |
| **Package 3** | Venue + lounge access (free) + any 2 × 45-min services | **A$300** |

Package mix assumption: 30/40/30 → **blended average A$250 per visit.**

Venue and lounge access are bundled free into every package — there is no separate seat fee. Ancillary revenue (spray tan A$60, retail, cafe) is in addition to package revenue.

### The GTT Clinical Pathway — How It Works

Blood collection is performed by employed phlebotomists under WDP/PathWest's Licensed Collection Centre arrangement. GTT Center Perth does not bill Medicare — WDP handles pathology billing.

| Time | Clinical Event | GTT Center Perth Role |
|------|---------------|----------------------|
| T=0 (arrival) | Fasting blood draw | Phlebotomist — Chair A or B |
| T+5 min | Client drinks 75g glucose in lounge | Staff assists, clocks draw window |
| T+15 min | Services begin | Treatment staff |
| T+60–75 min | 1-hour blood draw (5-min window) | Phlebotomist times draw between service transitions |
| T+120–130 min | Final blood draw, client discharged | Phlebotomist |

**No-interrupt rule:** Blood draws do not interrupt services. The phlebotomist schedules draws to coincide with natural service transitions — no client is paused mid-massage for a blood draw.

**8 clients concurrently** are managed in the AM window (Scenario B): staggered 20-minute arrivals, 2 phlebotomy chairs, 2 phlebotomists, verified timetable 07:40–12:25 (see `operations-manual.md`).

### PM Spa Packages — Second Revenue Stream

From Month 1, the centre runs standalone PM Spa Packages (12:00–18:00) for non-GTT clients: same Package 1/2/3 menu, no blood collection. Served by 1 PM Service Therapist (cross-trained massage + facial), ramping from 2 packages/day at launch to 6/day by Month 5.

This transforms a morning-only venue into a full-day operation — second revenue stream from identical fixed costs.
