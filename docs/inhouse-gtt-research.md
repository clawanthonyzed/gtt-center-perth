# GTT Center Perth — In-House GTT Feasibility Research
## Can We Run the Full 75g OGTT Without WDP/PathWest?
### Version 1.0 | June 2026 | Owner: Cora (Clinical)

---

## VERDICT: FEASIBLE — PHASED APPROACH REQUIRED

In-house GTT (collect + analyse + report on-site) is legally possible and clinically precedented in Australia. It requires NATA accreditation (ISO 15189), an Approved Pathology Laboratory (APL) registration with Medicare, and at minimum one qualified Clinical Scientist on staff. The process takes 12–18 months.

**Recommended strategy:** Launch under WDP LCC model immediately. Begin NATA accreditation in parallel from Month 3–6. Transition to in-house APL by Month 18.

---

## 1. WHAT "IN-HOUSE" MEANS

### Current Model (WDP LCC)
1. GTT Center Perth collects blood (T=0, T=60, T=120)
2. Specimens sent to WDP laboratory (external — courier or drop)
3. WDP analyses glucose, issues results
4. Results go to referring doctor via WDP system
5. GTT Center Perth never sees or communicates results
6. WDP bills Medicare — GTT Center Perth earns nothing from pathology

### In-House Model (APL)
1. GTT Center Perth collects blood (T=0, T=60, T=120)
2. GTT Center Perth centrifuges samples immediately on-site
3. GTT Center Perth analyses glucose on-site using NATA-accredited method
4. Clinical Scientist authorises results
5. GTT Center Perth reports results to referring doctor directly
6. GTT Center Perth bills Medicare item 66548 — A$19.90/test (bulk-billed, patient pays $0)
7. **Patient acquisition advantage:** "Free GTT + premium wellness" — unique in WA

## 2. DIAGNOSTIC STANDARDS — ADIPS 2025

**ADIPS updated its GDM diagnostic thresholds in 2025.** All GTT Center Perth clinical documents must use ADIPS 2025 values:

| Measurement | ADIPS 2014 threshold | **ADIPS 2025 threshold** | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fasting plasma glucose | ≥ 5.1 mmol/L | **≥ 5.3 mmol/L** | ↑ 0.2 |
| 1-hour plasma glucose | ≥ 10.0 mmol/L | **≥ 10.6 mmol/L** | ↑ 0.6 |
| 2-hour plasma glucose | ≥ 8.5 mmol/L | **≥ 9.0 mmol/L** | ↑ 0.5 |

ADIPS 2025 endorsed by: AACB, RCPA, RANZCOG, ADS, ACM.

**Action required:** Update gtt-test-reference.md and gtt-clinical-protocol.md to ADIPS 2025 thresholds. All patient-facing materials must use 2025 values only.

### What ADIPS Requires for Measurement Method

ADIPS mandates **venous plasma glucose** throughout. Capillary blood glucose (finger prick) is not acceptable for GDM diagnosis at any threshold. All measurements must be from a NATA-accredited laboratory using validated enzymatic methods.

## 3. POINT-OF-CARE TESTING — HARD NO FOR DIAGNOSIS

This path is permanently closed for diagnostic purposes.

**Why POCT cannot diagnose GDM:**
- Capillary glucose concentrations are consistently higher than venous plasma glucose across all OGTT timepoints — misclassification is systematic
- HemoCue Glucose 201RT: only 95.9–98.1% of values in "no risk zone" — insufficient for a diagnostic standard that affects pregnancy management
- Abbott i-STAT: not validated for GDM diagnostic thresholds
- Roche Cobas b 101: does not measure glucose (measures HbA1c and lipids only — not relevant)
- No Australian guideline or regulatory body has approved any POCT device for definitive GDM diagnosis

**What POCT can do:** Wellness screening with written disclosure that results are not for clinical diagnosis. GTT Center Perth could offer a POCT "wellness glucose screen" as a separate non-diagnostic service — but this has no clinical weight and cannot replace the OGTT for GDM purposes.

## 4. PRE-ANALYTICAL PROTOCOL — CRITICAL FROM DAY 1

**This must be implemented immediately under the WDP model, before any NATA work begins.**

The WA ORCHID study (PMC6888052) found that failure to centrifuge OGTT samples within 10 minutes causes in-vitro glycolysis that **misses 62% of GDM diagnoses**. Many collection centres courier samples without adequate pre-analytical handling, producing systematic underdiagnosis.

### GTT Center Perth Pre-Analytical Protocol (implement from Day 1)

| Step | Requirement | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Tube type | Sodium fluoride / potassium oxalate (NaF/Ox) — grey cap | Every draw |
| Inversion | Gently invert tube 8–10 times immediately after collection | Within 30 sec of draw |
| Storage | Place tube in ice-water slurry immediately | Within 1 min of draw |
| Centrifugation | Centrifuge at 2,000–3,000 × g for 10 min | Within 10 min of draw |
| Post-centrifuge | Separate plasma, transfer to secondary tube, store at 2–8°C | Within 15 min |
| Dispatch | Deliver to WDP within 4 hours of collection | Same morning |

**Equipment required (from Day 1, under WDP model):**
- Tabletop centrifuge capable of ≥2,000 × g: A$2,000–5,000 (e.g., Hettich EBA 20 or equivalent)
- Ice-water container (insulated box with ice packs): A$50–100
- Secondary plasma tubes (1.5mL micro-tubes): A$50/box of 500
- Fridge for plasma storage (existing specimen fridge at 2–8°C serves dual purpose)

This protocol differentiates GTT Center Perth from standard pathology collection centres and provides a strong clinical quality story.

## 5. NATA ACCREDITATION PATHWAY

### Which Standard

**ISO 15189** (Medical Laboratory accreditation) is the required pathway. This is administered jointly by NATA and RCPA for human pathology in Australia.

ISO 22870 (POCT) is a supplement to ISO 15189 — it cannot be obtained standalone. A business must first achieve ISO 15189 accreditation before ISO 22870 can apply to point-of-care devices within the lab scope.

### Process

| Stage | Activity | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Contact NATA for advisory visit; submit application | Month 3–6 |
| 2 | NATA advisory visit — gap analysis, Assessment Information Document (AID) issued | Month 4–7 |
| 3 | Build quality management system, SOPs, document control | Month 5–10 |
| 4 | Procure and qualify glucose analyzer | Month 5–8 |
| 5 | Hire Clinical Scientist (APP) and MLS | Month 6–9 |
| 6 | Internal quality runs, QC validation, method verification | Month 9–12 |
| 7 | Submit for NATA initial assessment | Month 12 |
| 8 | NATA on-site assessment; resolve non-conformances | Month 12–15 |
| 9 | NATA recommendation issued | Month 15–17 |
| 10 | Apply to Services Australia for APL + APA approval | Month 16–18 |
| 11 | In-house GTT operations begin | Month 18+ |

### Cost

## 6. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK — FEDERAL (NO WA-SPECIFIC BLOCKING LAW)

There is no separate WA pathology laboratory registration. The entire framework is federal:

| Legislation | What it does |
|---|---|
| Health Insurance Act 1973 (Cth) | Establishes APA/APL/APP framework; links lab approval to Medicare billing |
| Health Insurance Regulations 2018 (Cth) | Operational rules |
| Health Insurance (Accredited Pathology Laboratories — Approval) Principles 2017 | Mandates NATA as the accreditation body |
| NPAAC Standards (ACSQHC, 7th Ed 2023) | Technical standards for quality and supervision |

**Three approvals required to bill Medicare:**

| Approval | What | Who grants |
|---|---|---|
| **APL** — Accredited Pathology Laboratory | The laboratory itself is approved | Services Australia (after NATA accreditation) |
| **APA** — Approved Pathology Authority | The business entity that operates the APL | Services Australia |
| **APP** — Approved Pathology Practitioner | The individual who authorises results | Services Australia |

## 7. STAFFING — WHAT IS REQUIRED

### Current (LCC model): Phlebotomist only
The phlebotomist cannot operate a laboratory or authorise diagnostic results under any regulatory pathway.

### In-House APL model: Two additional roles required

| Role | Requirements | Annual Cost (WA) |
|---|---|---|
| **Clinical Scientist / APP** (Designated Person) | Minimum: relevant science degree + 5 years experience + FAACB, FAIMS, or FASM fellowship. This person authorises all results and oversees the QMS. Can be part-time or consulting arrangement. | A$100,000–140,000 FTE; A$30,000–50,000 part-time/consulting |
| **Medical Laboratory Scientist (MLS)** | Operates analyzer, performs daily QC, handles pre-analytical and post-analytical steps. Health Professionals Award MA000027 Level 3–4. | A$80,000–95,000 FTE |
| **Total additional staff cost** | | **A$120,000–180,000/year** |

**The Clinical Scientist must hold a fellowship qualification.** A standard Bachelor of Medical Laboratory Science (BMLS) does not qualify for the APP/Designated Person role. Recruitment for this role requires targeting AACB fellows or experienced senior clinical scientists considering a private practice move.

### Possible Hybrid

If a single highly qualified candidate holds both a fellowship AND is willing to operate the analyzer (i.e., a Clinical Scientist who also functions as MLS), this reduces to one hire. This is unusual but not impossible for a small-volume lab. Cost: A$100,000–130,000/year.

| Analyzer | Type | Sample | GDM-diagnostic quality | Purchase cost (AUD) | Annual consumables/QC |

| **Randox daytona+** | Benchtop clinical chemistry | Plasma/serum | Excellent — <1% bias vs YSI 2300. Broad test menu (glucose + HbA1c + lipids + more) | A$15,000–30,000 | A$10,000–20,000 |
| YSI 2300 STAT Plus | Gold standard (discontinued) | Whole blood/plasma | Was the reference method | Discontinued July 2021 | N/A |

**Recommendation:** EKF Biosen C-Line for Phase 1 in-house (lower capital, proven, Australian market presence). Randox daytona+ if expanding to multi-test menu (HbA1c, lipids) for a broader clinical offer.

**TGA registration:** Both EKF and Randox products are available in Australia through local distributors. Confirm TGA ARTG registration of the specific model before purchase.

## 9. MEDICARE BILLING

### MBS Item 66548

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| Item | 66548 |
| Description | 75g OGTT for GDM in pregnancy — glucose admin + ≥3 glucose measurements |
| Schedule fee | **A$19.90** |
| Medicare 75% benefit | **A$14.95** |
| Bulk-bill: patient pays | **A$0** |

**The A$19.90 schedule fee does not cover the cost of running the test.** It barely covers consumables. The business model value of Medicare billing is **patient acquisition**, not revenue:

- Patients who currently pay out-of-pocket for GTT (at centres not bulk-billing) would pay A$0 at GTT Center Perth
- "Free GTT + premium wellness" = the strongest possible value proposition in the Perth market
- The wellness upsell (massage, nails, scan, dietitian) becomes the entire revenue engine — no pathology cost for the patient

### Additional MBS Items Available to an APL

An APL billing for OGTT can also claim the collection fee if a nurse or phlebotomist employed by the APL performs the collection. Ancillary pathology items exist but are modest. The primary value is the bulk-billing positioning, not item stacking.

These precedents confirm: **small, single-site, pregnancy-focused private clinics in Australia do run NATA-accredited in-house laboratories and directly bill Medicare.** GTT Center Perth's model is not novel at the regulatory level — it maps directly onto the fertility clinic precedent.

No dedicated GTT-only private clinic with in-house pathology was identified. GTT Center Perth would be the first in Australia in this specific niche — which is the defining competitive moat.

## 11. WHAT GTT CENTER PERTH MUST NOT DO (without APL status)

| Action | Status | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Report glucose values to referring doctors as clinical results | **PROHIBITED** without APL | Health Insurance Act 1973 civil/criminal penalties |
| Bill Medicare for pathology | **PROHIBITED** without APL/APA/APP | Federal penalty |
| Use in-house analyzer results to inform GDM diagnosis | **PROHIBITED** without NATA-accredited scope | Clinical liability, regulatory breach |
| Operate a POCT device for wellness screening (non-diagnostic) | Permitted with appropriate disclosure | Low risk if disclosure is clear and results not used diagnostically |

## 12. PHASED STRATEGY

### Phase 1 — Launch Now (Month 1–18): WDP LCC Model
- Open under existing WDP Licensed Collection Centre arrangement
- Implement immediate centrifugation protocol (Day 1)
- Build patient base, prove wellness model, generate revenue
- No NATA delays, no capital risk for pathology

### Phase 2 — NATA Accreditation (Month 3–18): In Parallel
- Month 3–6: Engage NATA advisory visit. Identify Clinical Scientist/APP candidate.
- Month 6–9: Hire Clinical Scientist + MLS. Procure EKF Biosen C-Line or Randox daytona+.
- Month 9–12: Build quality management system, validate methods, run internal QC.
- Month 12–18: NATA assessment, Services Australia APL/APA approval.

### Phase 3 — In-House APL Live (Month 18+)
- Transition all OGTT analysis in-house
- Terminate WDP LCC arrangement (give appropriate contractual notice)
- Begin direct Medicare bulk-billing
- Market: "Perth's only free GTT — fully in-house, results available same morning"

### Phase 3 Cost Summary (additional vs LCC model)

| Item | Annual cost |
|---|---|
| Clinical Scientist (part-time or consulting) | A$40,000–60,000 |
| MLS (full-time) | A$85,000–95,000 |
| NATA annual membership + QMS | A$10,000–20,000 |
| Analyzer consumables + QC | A$10,000–20,000 |
| **Total additional annual cost** | **A$145,000–195,000** |
| Medicare revenue (20 GTTs/day × 220 days × A$14.95 benefit) | ~A$65,780 |
| **Net additional cost after Medicare** | **~A$80,000–130,000/year** |

This net cost is offset by: increased patient volume from "free GTT" positioning, higher wellness conversion from lower patient cost barrier, and the competitive moat of being the only in-house GTT facility in WA (and Australia in this standalone format).
