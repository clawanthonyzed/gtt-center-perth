### About GTT Center Perth

GTT Center Perth is a concept currently in the planning/feasibility stage for Western Australia's first dedicated Glucose Tolerance Test (GTT) wellness venue. The concept pairs clinical-grade blood collection with premium pregnancy wellness services (massage, nails, hair, beauty) during the mandatory 2-3 hour GTT wait.

*Note for whoever posts this ad: "GTT Center Perth" is currently a working/placeholder name, not a finalised brand — check `brand-guide.md` for current naming status before external use, since a premium brand name may be created before this role is actually recruited.*

This is not a standard day spa or a standard pathology lab — it is a new category combining both, built specifically around the pregnant client experience.

The venue will operate as a Licensed Collection Centre under a partner pathology provider's NATA accreditation (pathology partnership in progress — see `reed-partnerships.md`).

---

### Role Overview

The Venue Manager is the on-site Managing Director and operational backbone of GTT Center Perth — the single point of accountability for daily operations, staff supervision, clinical escalation, financial oversight, and partner liaison. This is the **first hire** made once recruitment begins, and every other role (phlebotomist, reception, treatment staff) reports into this position directly or indirectly.

Unlike a typical venue manager role, this position carries genuine clinical-adjacent responsibility: this person will be the venue's designated emergency first-responder and EpiPen holder, meaning the venue cannot legally or safely open without this role filled and appropriately credentialed (current First Aid certification required before Day 1).

You will work in a small, purpose-driven team alongside employed phlebotomists, a receptionist, and wellness practitioners (massage, nails, hair, beauty) — all directly employed by GTT Center Perth, not subtenants or room-renters.

### Key Responsibilities

**Operations (primary)**
- Venue operations: opening/closing procedures, daily checklist, lounge and collection-room readiness
- Staff management: rostering, performance, Fair Work compliance, onboarding coordination
- Booking oversight: Fresha (or equivalent) calendar management, service scheduling
- Quality: customer feedback review, service standard enforcement, Google review monitoring

**Financial**
- Weekly P&L review and reporting to Anthony
- Day-to-day payment approver for expenses under A$2,000 (see `financial-setup.md` Financial Gates)
- Payroll oversight in coordination with Bruno (Finance agent)/accountant

**Clinical / Safety**
- Emergency first-responder for any clinical or customer incident (fainting, adverse reaction, obstetric emergency escalation) — see `emergency-plan.md`
- EpiPen holder — must hold current First Aid certification before the venue opens; this credential requirement makes the role a genuine critical-path hire, not just an administrative one
- Fire warden (primary)
- Clinical escalation contact for phlebotomist and treatment staff — see `gtt-clinical-protocol.md`

**HR / Compliance**
- HR disciplinary presence: verbal warnings, show-cause meetings, terminations (with Fern/HR agent support and solicitor engagement for anything serious) — see `hr-framework.md`
- WorkSafe WA and Fair Work compliance oversight

**Partnerships**
- WDP/PathWest (or other pathology partner) liaison — monthly operational review, same-day escalation of any clinical incident
- Quarterly visits to referring practices (midwives, OBs) alongside marketing/partnership agents

## PART 4 — INTERVIEW PROCESS

### Screening (phone — 15 min)

Conducted by Fern (HR agent preparing questions) / **Anthony (interim interview authority)** — since the Venue Manager role does not yet exist at the point this hire is being made, there is no existing Venue Manager to conduct this interview. Anthony (or a delegate Anthony names) runs this process directly. This is a structural difference from `phlebotomist-job-posting.md`'s process, where the Venue Manager (already hired by that point) conducts phlebotomist interviews.

Questions:
1. Tell me about your experience managing a small team (5-15 people) — what setting, how long, what was your role?
2. Have you managed P&L or expense-approval responsibilities before? Describe the scale.
3. Do you currently hold a First Aid certificate, or are you willing to obtain one before starting?
4. Why are you interested in a founding operational role at a new venue concept, rather than an established business?
5. Are you comfortable with early starts (7:00am) five days a week?
6. Describe a time you had to handle a difficult staff or customer situation directly — what did you do?

**Screening pass criteria:** Management experience confirmed at appropriate scale, financial comfort confirmed, First Aid willingness/status confirmed, genuine interest in the founding/ground-up nature of the role, no obvious disqualifying factors.

### In-person interview (1 hour)

**Interview panel:** Anthony

**Assessment areas:**

| Area | Questions |
|---|---|
| Operational judgement | "Walk me through how you'd handle a morning where two staff call in sick and you have a full booking calendar." |
| Financial literacy | "How would you decide whether an unplanned A$1,500 expense is worth approving on the spot versus escalating?" |
| Crisis/incident response | "A client feels faint during a treatment. Walk me through what you do, in order." |
| HR/people management | "Describe how you've handled a underperforming staff member — what was the process, and what was the outcome?" |
| Partner relationship management | "How would you approach a monthly check-in with an external partner (e.g., a pathology provider) whose service is critical to daily operations?" |
| Founding-role fit | "This role has no existing playbook to follow on Day 1 — what makes you comfortable building the operational rhythm from scratch?" |

### Reference checks (2 required)

- Must include at least one reference from a role with direct staff-management responsibility
- Ask referee: "Would you re-hire this person into a management role?" "How did they handle a genuine crisis or difficult decision under pressure?" "How reliable and consistent were they with day-to-day operational discipline (opening/closing, reporting, financial process)?"
