## Privacy Policy

**GTT Center Perth** (operated by YETI Holding Trust, ABN [to be inserted], trustee YETI Tipi Holdings PTY LTD) is committed to protecting the privacy of all individuals whose personal information we collect and handle.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and disclose your personal information, and your rights regarding that information. We comply with the **Privacy Act 1988 (Cth)** and the **Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)**.

---

## 1. What information we collect

### General personal information
- Name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Suburb / postcode

### Sensitive information (health information)
- Due date / gestational age (from waitlist and booking forms)
- Medical history relevant to your appointment (pregnancy conditions, medications, allergies — from intake and consent forms)
- Pathology request forms (from your referring doctor)
- Clinical appointment records (blood draw times, specimen dispatch logs — retained for pathology compliance)

### Payment information
- Credit card details (processed by Fresha Pay — we do not store raw card numbers)

### Website information
- IP address, browser type (via website analytics — aggregated, not personally identifiable)

---

## 2. Why we collect this information

We collect personal information to:
- Process and manage your appointment booking
- Coordinate your GTT (blood collection) appointment with our phlebotomist
- Dispatch your blood specimens to our pathology partner ([WDP/PathWest]) for analysis
- Provide you with wellness services (massage, nail treatment, brow services)
- Send you appointment reminders and post-visit follow-up communications
- Respond to your enquiries
- Comply with our legal obligations (health records, Fair Work, WorkCover)

We collect health information (sensitive information) specifically to:
- Ensure safe delivery of wellness services (massage contraindications, allergies)
- Comply with pathology collection regulatory requirements (NATA, WDP/PathWest protocols)
- Protect your safety during your appointment

**We will not use your information for marketing unless you have provided express consent.**

---

## 3. How we collect information

We collect information:
- Through our website (waitlist form, contact form, booking enquiry form)
- Through our booking system (Fresha) when you make an appointment
- In person at the venue (consent form, intake form, pathology request form)
- From your referring doctor (name, gestational age, requested tests — on the pathology request form)
- Via email or phone when you contact us

---

## 4. How we store and protect your information

Your personal information is stored:
- **Fresha** (booking system): encrypted, cloud-based, SOC 2 compliant
- **Resend** (email communications): used for appointment reminders and newsletters
- **Google Workspace / physical records** (clinical logs): secured in locked storage at the venue
- **WDP/PathWest systems** (for specimens dispatched): subject to their own privacy policy

**Security measures:**
- Access to patient records is restricted to GTT Center Perth staff with a legitimate need
- Physical clinical records are stored in a locked cabinet accessible only to the Managing Director and phlebotomist
- We require all staff to sign confidentiality agreements

**We retain your information for:**
- Clinical pathology records: 7 years from last appointment (legal requirement)
- Booking and contact information: 3 years from last contact
- Financial records: 7 years (ATO requirements)

---

## 5. Who we share your information with

We will only share your personal information with:

| Recipient | Purpose | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| WDP / PathWest (pathology partner) | Specimen processing and result reporting to your referring doctor | Necessary for the service you have requested |
| Your referring doctor / midwife | Pathology results are reported to them by WDP/PathWest — NOT by GTT Center Perth | You consented to the GTT referral by bringing the request form |
| Fresha (booking software) | Appointment management | Data processing agreement in place |
| Resend (email platform) | Appointment reminders and communications | You provided your email for this purpose |
| Legal and regulatory authorities | As required by law (e.g., OAIC, courts, regulators) | Legal obligation |

**We do NOT sell your personal information.**  
**We do NOT share your information with advertisers.**  
**We do NOT disclose health information to your employer or insurance company.**

## 6. Your rights

Under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth), you have the right to:

**Access your information:** Request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.

**Correct your information:** Request that we correct any inaccurate, outdated, or incomplete information.

**Complain:** Lodge a complaint with us if you believe we have breached your privacy.

**Withdraw consent:** Withdraw your consent to us holding your information (subject to our legal obligations to retain clinical records).

**To exercise these rights:** Contact us at [email address] or [phone number]. We will respond within 30 days.

---

## 7. Health information — additional protections

Health information is **sensitive information** under APP 3. We take extra care with health information:
- We collect it only where necessary
- We do not use it for purposes beyond your care and our legal obligations
- We restrict access to authorised staff only
- We will never disclose your health information to a third party without your consent, except where required by law

---

## 8. Data breaches

We take data security seriously. In the event of a data breach that is likely to cause serious harm:
- We will notify the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) within 30 days (mandatory notification under the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme)
- We will notify affected individuals as required
