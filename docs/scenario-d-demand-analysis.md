# Scenario D — Is There Enough Clientele? (330 GTT visits/month)

**Date:** 2026-07-17

## The market exists, in theory

From `feasibility.md`'s own market sizing (AIHW Perinatal Data 2024):

| Figure | Value |
|---|---|
| Annual births, Perth metro | ~28,000/year |
| Weekly pregnancies in the 24–28 week GTT window | ~1,100/week (whole metro) |
| Realistic capturable catchment (Subiaco/Nedlands/West Perth corridor, 15km radius) | **~200–250 GTT tests/week** |
| = per month | ~880–1,100 tests/month in catchment |

## What Scenario D actually needs

15 clients/day × 22 trading days = **330 GTT visits/month.**

**`feasibility.md` already flagged this exact number: 330/month requires a 30–37% capture rate of the entire realistic catchment.** That's not a small ask — it means roughly 1 in 3 of every GTT test happening in the whole Subiaco/Nedlands/West Perth corridor would need to come through this one venue.

## Context for how big a bar that is

| Scenario | Visits/month | % of catchment captured |
|---|---|---|
| Scenario B (launch, 8/day) | 176 | ~16–20% |
| Scenario C (current, 10/day) | 220 | ~20–25% |
| **Scenario D (3rd phlebotomist, 15/day)** | **330** | **~30–37%** |

Scenario D isn't just "more clients on the same growth curve" — it's asking for meaningfully deeper market penetration than the current model, not just more capacity to serve demand that's already shown up.

## What actually gates this — referral partnerships, not scheduling

Capture rate depends almost entirely on GP/midwife referral relationships, since that's how clients are directed to a specific collection venue. Current state, per this session and `pathology-partnership-brief.md`/`reed-partnerships.md`:
- WDP: enquiry sent, no reply yet
- PathWest, Clinipath: not yet contacted
- No midwife/OB referral partnerships confirmed yet — `operations-manual.md` names Poppy (Marketing) as owning "quarterly check-in, visits top 5 referring practices," but that's an ongoing-operations cadence, not evidence of current volume

**Bottom line: the total addressable market is large enough to support Scenario D's 330/month in theory, but reaching a 30–37% capture rate needs real, proven referral penetration that doesn't exist yet.** The scheduling and P&L case for the 3rd phlebotomist is solid (see `scenario-d-investigation.md`) — the actual risk isn't "can we physically serve 15 clients," it's "can we generate enough referral volume to fill 15 slots a day." Recommend treating Scenario D as the target to grow into once Scenario C (220/month, ~20-25% capture) is consistently full and the referral pipeline shows it can sustain growth beyond that — not a Day 1 commitment alongside the 3rd hire.
