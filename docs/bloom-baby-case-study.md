1. Email Bloom Baby via their website contact form — introduce GTT Center Perth concept

4. Proposed terms:

**Key negotiation point:** GTT Center Perth expects to refer 3-5 scan bookings per day (GTT patients). Bloom Baby must be able to serve this demand during morning hours.

## In-House 3D Scanning Operation (Phase 2)

### When to Bring In-House
- When subtenant cannot serve volume (>4 scans/day consistently)
- When subtenant agreement ends or is not renewed
- When own equipment ROI is demonstrated (break-even ~8 scans/week)

### Equipment Selection

**Recommended path:** Refurbished SonoScape S20 or Mindray DC-80 from Australian TGA-registered distributor.

| Model | Source | Price (AUD) | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------------|-------|
| SonoScape S20 (refurb) | Sonologic AU (sonologic.com.au) | A$8,000-18,000 | Strong 3D/4D, Australian support |
| Mindray DC-80 (refurb) | Mindray AU distributor | A$12,000-25,000 | Excellent brand recognition |
| Samsung WS80A (refurb) | International refurb dealers | A$18,000-30,000 | Gold standard; best marketing appeal |

**NEVER purchase:**
- Unregistered Chinese import direct (TGA compliance violation)
- Equipment not supported by an Australian sponsor/distributor

**TGA requirement:** All ultrasound machines are Class IIa/IIb medical devices. Must be listed on the Australian Register of Therapeutic Goods (ARTG) before supply in Australia. Always confirm ARTG listing with supplier before purchase.

### Key Specs to Request

- **Probe:** Convex/curved array, 3.5-5 MHz, dedicated OB probe (obstetric)
- **Modes:** 3D surface rendering, 4D live (minimum 25 fps), colour Doppler
- **Display:** Minimum 15-inch internal monitor
- **Output:** HDMI to external TV (60-inch+ for client viewing room), USB/SD for image/video capture
- **Software:** Measurement tools (BPD, FL), session recording, HD rendering (HDLive or equivalent)

### Scan Room Requirements

- **Size:** 18-20 sqm minimum (patient recliner + family seating for 4-6 + equipment + TV)
- **Lighting:** Dimmable/blackout — low ambient light is essential for image quality
- **TV:** 60-inch minimum on wall for family to view scan in real time (emotional experience = key differentiator)
- **Seating:** Comfortable reclining chair for patient + 4-6 chairs for family/friends (differentiator: competitors rarely accommodate large groups)
- **Decor:** Warm, soft, premium — NOT clinical. No harsh medical aesthetics.

### Staff Training

**Who trains:**
- UltrasoundTrainers.com — Perth WA training sessions available (hands-on, private sessions available)
- Budget: A$1,500-4,000 per operator
- Supervised scans required: 50-200 scans before working independently

**What training covers:**
- Machine operation and probe handling
- 3D/4D image optimisation (adjusting depth, gain, angle for best face detail)
- Understanding gestational age windows (optimal: 28-32 weeks)
- Client communication scripts: what to say, what NOT to say
- Scope of practice: keepsake only — never make diagnostic statements
- Documentation: consent forms, scope of service disclaimers

**What NOT to teach (strict protocol):**
- Any interpretation of fetal anatomy as normal/abnormal
- Any comparison to prior clinical scans
- Any comment on fetal position or estimated weight (diagnostic territory)
- "The baby looks healthy" — NEVER say this

### Consenting Framework

Before every scan, patient signs consent stating:
1. This is a keepsake service — NOT a diagnostic scan
2. This does not replace clinical ultrasound (anatomy scan, growth scan, etc.)
3. Image quality may vary based on fetal position, maternal body habitus, gestational age
4. Scan operator is NOT a sonographer or clinician — no medical statements will be made
5. If the patient is concerned about fetal wellbeing, they should contact their GP/OB/midwife

## 3D Scan: GTT Wait Window Scheduling

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| T=0 | Patient arrives, fasting draw + glucose drink |
| T+15 | Patient enters lounge; can commence scan |
| T+15 to T+45 | Standard 3D scan (30 min) — patient reclined, physically at rest |
| T+75 | Return for 1-hour blood draw (5 min) |
| T+80 | Can resume other service after draw |
| T+120 | Return for 2-hour blood draw (final) |

**Best practice:** Schedule 3D scan as FIRST service after T+15. Patient is emotional, eager to see the baby. This is the highest-value moment of their visit.

**After T+45:** Patient can move to another service (nails, brows, massage, etc.) before 2-hour draw.

## Revenue Analysis: Subtenant vs In-House

| Scenario | Revenue/year | Cost/year | Net/year |
|----------|-------------|----------|----------|
| Bloom Baby subtenant (A$300/week room rental) | A$15,600 | A$0 | A$15,600 |
| In-house (8 scans/day x A$110 x 250 days) | A$220,000 | A$65,000 (scanner + staff) | A$155,000 |
| In-house (3 scans/day x A$110 x 250 days) | A$82,500 | A$65,000 | A$17,500 |

**Break-even for in-house:** 8 scans per week (1.6/day) covers staff cost alone. Break-even including scanner depreciation (A$15,000 over 5 years = A$3,000/year): ~12 scans/week.

**Trigger:** When scan demand consistently exceeds 12/week, or when Bloom Baby subtenant cannot serve volume, switch to in-house.
