## Overview

This document covers services beyond the core GTT + beauty offering. These services make GTT Center Perth a comprehensive maternity wellness hub rather than just a test-day convenience. Many of these are low-cost to implement (tablet-based, contractor/subtenant model) but add significant perceived value and differentiation.

## 1. Perinatal Mental Health Screening

### What It Is
The Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS) is a 10-question validated self-report screening tool — the Australian gold standard for perinatal mental health screening. It takes 5-10 minutes to complete. Antenatal use (before birth) is equally valid as postnatal use.

### Why This Matters for GTT Center Perth
The 2-hour GTT wait creates a rare opportunity: women are seated, typically alone, and often anxious about the test. Research shows anxiety peaks during this wait. Offering opt-in mental health screening:
- Catches women who may not otherwise disclose to their GP
- Positions GTT Center Perth as a genuinely caring health service (not just a spa)
- Zero clinical staff required for the screening itself
- Creates referral relationship with WACPPS, Gidget Foundation, and local GPs

### Who Can Administer It
The EPDS is self-administered. No clinical qualification is required to offer it via tablet. Any staff member can show a patient to the tablet or share the QR code.

**Positive screen protocol (mandatory):**
- Score 10+: Reception staff hand the patient a resource card with referral contacts and ask if they would like reception to call WACPPS on their behalf
- Score 13+: Same as above, ask if they would like a warm verbal handoff
- Q10 any response: Immediate sensitive conversation; have PANDA (1300 726 306) and Lifeline (13 11 14) resource card ready; offer private space in venue
- Staff are NOT clinicians — the role is warm facilitation and referral, not clinical assessment

### Platform
**iCOPE** (icope.org.au) — Australia's national digital perinatal mental health screening platform by COPE.
- QR code completion on any device in the waiting room
- Contains EPDS + Antenatal Risk Questionnaire (ANRQ)
- Contact COPE directly to confirm free access eligibility vs private subscription
- Alternative: Download the validated EPDS PDF and load onto a Jotform form — free, self-administered

### Referral Pathway (WA)
- **WACPPS** (WA Centre for Perinatal Mental Health and Parenting Support): (08) 9496 0460 — telephone assessment within 2 business days
- **Gidget Foundation**: Free specialist perinatal counselling — face-to-face Harrisdale + Joondalup + national telehealth (Start Talking program)
- **KEMH SPIMHP**: Statewide Perinatal and Infant Mental Health Program — poster available from KEMH for display
- **PANDA** (Perinatal Anxiety and Depression Australia): 1300 726 306
- **Beyond Blue**: 1300 22 4636
- **Local GP cards**: Print a laminated resource with 3-5 bulk-billing GPs within 5km of venue

### Cost to Implement
- iCOPE: Free (confirm eligibility) or low subscription
- Tablet: existing iPad (A$500-700)
- Printed resource cards: A$50-100 for design + print
- Staff training (30 min): One team meeting
- **Total: A$100-800**

### Revenue Model
- Screening is complimentary — included in lounge experience
- Builds trust and community reputation
- Creates warm referral relationship with WACPPS and Gidget (reciprocal referrals from them expected over time)

## 2. GDM (Gestational Diabetes Mellitus) Management

### The Opportunity
18% of GTT customers will receive a positive GDM diagnosis from their GP (24-48 hours after the test). These women face:
- Dietary changes
- Blood glucose monitoring (4-7x daily)
- Possible insulin
- Anxiety and confusion about what GDM means

GTT Center Perth has a **captive GDM-positive population** — if a GDM-positive woman booked at the center, she has an existing relationship. The center should be the first call after a positive result.

### What the Center Can Offer (Non-Clinical)

**Immediately (no additional qualification needed):**
- GDM education session (group or individual): "What is GDM, what to expect" — scripted, evidence-based, written by Cora agent
- GDM resource pack: NDSS "GD and Me" booklets (free bulk supply from NDSS), Baker Institute GDM fact sheet
- Blood glucose monitor demonstration (education only — not prescription)
- CGM retail: Freestyle Libre sensors (~A$102/sensor, 14-day) — high margin, high demand
- NDSS registration assistance: staff walk patient through registration at ndss.com.au or 1800 637 700
- Low-GI snack products: stock and retail GDM-appropriate snacks and recipe books

**With APD on staff (Month 3+):**
- Individual GDM meal planning consultation (APD required)
- GDM-specific dietary assessment
- Medicare rebate path: GP Management Plan (MBS item 81110, ~A$58 rebate per session — up to 5 sessions/year)

### NDSS for the Center
- GTT Center Perth cannot become an NDSS access point (pharmacies + accredited providers only)
- BUT: staff can explain NDSS, distribute registration forms, direct women to ndss.com.au
- NDSS provides free patient materials in bulk — contact NDSS for bulk supply

### Retail Products for GDM Customers
| Product | Source | Retail price | Margin |
|---------|--------|-------------|--------|
| Freestyle Libre 2 sensor (14-day) | Diabetes Supply Group, HealthWarehouse | A$100-110 | 20-30% |

## 3. Belly Casting

### What It Is
A 3D commemorative keepsake mold of the pregnant belly (and optionally breasts/hands) made using plaster bandage strips applied wet to the skin. Result: a personal art piece the family keeps for life.

### Why It Fits the GTT Wait
- Active time: 45-60 minutes (fits within the 95-minute service window)
- Client stays semi-reclined throughout — compliant with GTT rest protocol
- Best timing: 32-36 weeks — aligns with peak GTT testing window (24-32 weeks is core, but 32-36 weeks is a strong secondary market)
- One of the highest-margin services in the menu (COGS under A$30; retail A$220-280)

### Process
1. Client undresses to waist; skin coated with Vaseline (barrier)
2. Plaster gauze strips applied in overlapping layers (3-4 layers thick)
3. Plaster sets: 20-30 minutes (fast-set strips: 5-15 minutes active wait)
4. Cast carefully lifted off
5. Client wipes down; cast taken home to dry fully over 24-48 hours
6. Optional: return for painting/decoration session (additional revenue)

**Total active session time:** 45-60 minutes including prep and cleanup

### Materials (Cost Per Session)
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Plaster bandage rolls (6-8 rolls) | A$12-18 |
| Vaseline (small tub, shared supply) | A$0.50 |
| Cling wrap (barrier alternative for sensitive skin) | A$1 |
| Disposable tray (for water) | A$1 |
| Paint (if doing basic colour coat in-venue) | A$3-5 |
| **Total COGS** | **A$17-25** |

### Pricing
| Service | Price |
|---------|-------|
| Belly cast (white, unpainted, take-home) | A$220 |
| Belly cast + basic painted finish | A$275 |
| Belly + breast cast | A$280 |
| Decoration upgrade (client returns for painting session) | +A$75 |

### Training for Staff
No formal qualification. 1-day training:
- Australasian Doula College offers belly casting add-on workshops
- YouTube/online: "professional belly casting tutorial" — adequate for confident practitioners
- Practice on: a willing staff member, yoga/exercise ball, or silicone belly prosthetic

### Safety Notes
- Plaster generates mild heat as it sets — disclose to client before starting
- Not recommended before 28 weeks or after 38 weeks
- Check for latex allergy (some plaster bandage brands contain latex — use latex-free)
- Ensure client is hydrated; have water and snacks ready for afterward
- Optimal window: 32-36 weeks (strong belly definition, maximum keepsake value)

### Safety in GTT Context
- Do NOT offer on GTT test day if the physical activity of the casting (mild position changes) would stress the patient
- Separate booking preferred: belly casting as a standalone session or afternoon booking at the venue
- OR carefully scheduled during the 60-120min window when glucose is being processed and patient is well-rested

## 4. Maternity Photography + Automated Photo Booth

### Traditional Maternity Photography (Referral or Subtenant)
- Average Perth 1-hour session: ~A$395 (Perth is 13% above national average)
- GTT Center Perth is NOT a photography studio — photography is best offered as a referral or subtenant arrangement
- Option: Partner with 1-2 Perth maternity photographers who offer sessions in-venue (afternoon slots)
- Revenue model: 20% referral fee or room hire at A$100-150/session

### Self-Service Photo Station (Simple Implementation — Launch)
A styled backdrop wall with good lighting for self-directed belly photos.

**Equipment needed:**
- Professional backdrop (cream, white, or botanical): A$100-300
- 2 softbox lights on stands: A$300-600
- Ring light on stand: A$150-300
- Mirror (full-length, angled): A$100-200
- iPad on tripod with remote shutter app: A$50-100

**Total: A$700-1,500**

**How it works:**
- During the GTT wait, patient can use the photo station for self-directed belly photos
- They take photos on their own phone (no privacy concerns; no staff needed)
- Printed colour photos available at reception: A$5-15/print
- QR code nearby: link to professional maternity photographers in Perth for full sessions

### Full Automated Photo Booth (Future Option)
For a fully automated booth:
- DSLR/mirrorless on tether + kiosk touchscreen + dye-sub printer: A$5,000-10,000
- Photo booth software (Snappic, Simple Booth, Darkroom Booth): A$200-600/year
- Total setup: A$5,200-10,600
- Revenue: A$20-30/session (automatic) — 5 sessions/day = A$100-150/day = A$25,000-37,500/year
- **ROI: 6-12 months payback at 5 sessions/day**

**Recommendation:** Launch with simple styled backdrop (A$700-1,500). Add automated booth in Month 6 if demand supports it.

## 5. Men's Preconception Health

### Why Include It
- Male factors account for 40-50% of fertility issues
- It takes ~3 months to produce new sperm — male preconception health in the 90 days before conception directly affects outcomes
- GTT Center Perth is women-focused, but PARTNERS are present (in the cafe/waiting area)
- A "partner preconception check" while waiting can be a high-value add-on

### Positioning
- GTT Center Perth is a **women's safe space first** — primary experience is for the pregnant woman
- Men's preconception health is offered as an **add-on for the waiting partner**, not a competing service
- Separate, brief consultation or self-directed digital assessment (tablet-based)

### What to Offer (Non-Clinical)

**Phase 1 (Month 6+) — Self-Directed:**
- Digital men's preconception health checklist (tablet): lifestyle audit (diet, weight, alcohol, smoking, heat exposure, supplements)
- Resource pack: "Healthy Father Healthy Future" (yourfertility.org.au materials — free)
- Supplement retail: zinc, CoQ10, folate (methylfolate), vitamin D, omega-3 — strong evidence base for male fertility
- Referral card: "See your GP for a semen analysis + hormone panel" — simple, actionable

**Phase 2 (Month 12+) — With Naturopath:**
- Contract a naturopath for 1-2 sessions/week (afternoon)
- Male preconception consultation: 60-90 min, A$150-200
- No medical licence required; naturopaths legally practice preconception care in Australia

### Revenue Model
- Supplement retail: A$500-1,500/month once established (30-50% margin)
- Naturopath consultation: room hire fee or revenue share
- Partner goodwill: men who feel included are more likely to encourage their partner to return for postnatal services

## 6. Birth Plan Creation (Tablet-Based)

### What It Is
A birth plan is a written document stating a woman's preferences for labour, birth, and postnatal care. It is created by the woman (not a clinician) and shared with her care team.

### Implementation at GTT Center Perth
- 3 iPads in the lounge area: one pre-loaded with birth plan creation app/form
- Women complete their birth plan during the 2-hour GTT wait (20-30 minutes)
- Email the plan to themselves OR print on-site

**Apps/Platforms:**
- **Birth Plan Plus** (birthplanplus.com): dedicated app, tablet-compatible
- **healthdirect.gov.au/making-a-birth-plan**: Government-backed, free template
- **Custom Jotform form**: Build a branded GTT Center Perth birth plan form (Grace/Ivy agents)

### Printing Options and Pricing
| Format | Cost to venue | Charge to customer |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Digital only (emailed) | A$0 | Free |
| B&W print (1-2 pages) | A$0.20 | A$2 |
| Colour print | A$1.00 | A$5 |
| Premium (colour on card stock, plastic sleeve) | A$3-5 | A$15 |

### Legal/Liability
- Customer writes their own preferences — no clinical liability on the center
- The birth plan is not a medical order
- Add disclaimer: "This birth plan represents your personal preferences. All medical decisions rest with your healthcare team."
- Standard Privacy Act 1988 compliance: do not store personal data beyond the session unless consented

## 7. Lactation Consultant (Future Service — Month 4+)

### Why Include It
- Breastfeeding challenges are the most common postnatal concern
- Women who had GDM have a higher rate of breastfeeding difficulty
- A lactation consultant creates a natural postnatal return visit to the venue

### Qualification Required: IBCLC
**International Board Certified Lactation Consultant (IBCLC)** — the gold standard in Australia.
- Governed by IBLCE (Asia Pacific office, Queensland)
- WA body: College of Lactation Consultants WA (lactationwest.org.au)
- Requires: health professional background (nursing, midwifery) + 300-1,000 clinical hours + IBLCE exam

**CLC (Certified Lactation Counsellor)** — lower qualification, US-based ALPP accreditation. Less stringent; does not carry same credibility. Not Medicare rebatable.

**Recommendation:** Hire or contract an IBCLC. If the IBCLC is also an endorsed midwife, consultations within 7 weeks post-birth are Medicare rebatable (significant upside for client uptake).

### Perth IBCLC Options (Contract)
- Cherished Parenting Services (cherishedparenting.com.au)
- The Gentle Village (thegentlevillage.com.au) — also telehealth
- Bundle and Me (bundleandme.com.au)
- College of Lactation Consultants WA directory: lactationwest.org.au/private-practitioners

### Pricing
| Service | Duration | Price | Medicare rebate (if endorsed midwife IBCLC) |
|---------|----------|-------|---------------------------------------------|
| Initial clinic-based consultation | 60 min | A$150-200 | ~A$158-240 rebate within 7 weeks post-birth |
| Follow-up consultation | 30-45 min | A$90-130 | Partial rebate |

### Business Model
- IBCLC contractor: room hire at A$50-100/day or A$200-300/week
- IBCLC brings own clients + accepts GTT Center Perth postnatal referrals
- No direct revenue sharing needed — room hire income is the center's benefit
- Builds a postnatal service stream (women return to the venue postnatally)

## 9. Women-Focused Space Policy

GTT Center Perth is designed as a **women-focused/safe space**.

**What this means:**
- Primary experience areas (treatment rooms, massage rooms, lounge) are women-only
- Lockers/bathrooms: women-only
- Staff providing services: ideally women practitioners (not enforced by law, but strongly preferred by target market)

**Partners:**
- Welcome in the CAFE/SNACK area only (near the entrance)
- Not in treatment rooms or the main lounge
- Comfortable partner seating near the food and beverage station
- Partner experience: comfortable seating, WiFi, phone charging, snacks and drinks available
- Signage: "Patient lounge — quiet zone for our guests. Partners and visitors: please use the cafe area."

**Children:**
- Recommended NOT to attend (customers are there for self-care; children disrupt the calm environment)
- Exception: infants in arms (postnatal return visits)
- Expansion: future children/family experiences considered in Centre 2+ planning

**Male practitioners:**
- No restriction under law, but majority of pregnant women prefer female practitioners
- Job postings should not explicitly exclude male applicants (discrimination risk) but can describe "client-preferred female-led team" in the job description as a genuine occupational requirement
