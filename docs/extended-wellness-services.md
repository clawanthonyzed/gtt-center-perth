## 1. GDM (Gestational Diabetes) Awareness — Simplified

### What the Centre Offers

18% of GTT clients will receive a positive GDM diagnosis from their GP (24–48 hrs after the test). GTT Center Perth supports these women with education and resources — not clinical management.

**What staff CAN do (no qualification required):**
- Welcome GDM-positive clients back to the centre for a dedicated session
- Hand out free GDM resource packs (printed handouts from NDSS and Baker Institute — obtained in bulk)
- Sell CGM sensors and GDM-appropriate snacks (see retail below)
- Introduce client to the monthly/fortnightly GDM information session

**What staff CANNOT do:**
- Give dietary advice or meal plans (requires APD)
- Interpret blood glucose readings (clinical)
- Register clients with NDSS (their responsibility)
- Prescribe, recommend specific medications, or advise on insulin

### Monthly/Fortnightly GDM Information Session

**Format:** External presenter comes to GTT Center Perth, group session (lounge/multi-purpose room)
- Presenter: Accredited Practising Dietitian (APD), diabetes educator, or pharmacist — paid per session
- Cost to venue: A$150–300/session (contractor fee)
- Content: "What is GDM, what to expect, how to manage blood glucose, food basics, device demonstration"
- Device demonstration: show how a CGM sensor (Freestyle Libre) and a blood glucose meter work — education only, no prescription
- Session duration: 45–60 min
- Charge to client: A$0 (included in GTT package goodwill) or A$20–30 (optional)

**When to launch:** Month 3+ when there is an established pool of GDM-diagnosed return clients.

### GDM Retail

**Not offered:** NDSS registration, CGM prescription, meal plan services, APD consultations.

## 2. Belly Casting — Standalone Service

### Confirmed: Outside GTT Window

Belly casting is a 60–75 min service. It is a **standalone booking only** — not scheduled during the GTT test window.

- Best booking: afternoon or a separate morning appointment (not on GTT test day)
- Optimal gestational window: 32–36 weeks

### Process

1. Client semi-undresses to waist; skin coated with Vaseline (barrier)
2. Plaster gauze strips applied in overlapping layers
3. Plaster sets: fast-dry 5–15 min / traditional 20–30 min
4. Cast carefully lifted off; client wipes down
5. Cast taken home to dry fully (24–48 hrs)
6. Optional return visit: painting/decoration session (+A$75)

**Fast-dry vs traditional:** Fast-dry saves ~5–15 min in setting time only. Overall session and final curing time nearly identical. Fast-dry materials cost slightly more. Recommend fast-dry for client comfort (shorter wait during setting).

### Pricing

Perth market comparison: Natural Beginnings Perth charges A$770 for premium belly bowl (includes acacia stand + mosaic). Raw cast pricing nationally: A$150–395.

| Service | Duration | Price |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Belly cast (white, unpainted, take-home) | 60 min | A$250 |
| Belly cast + basic painted finish | 75 min | A$310 |
| Belly + breast cast | 75 min | A$330 |
| Decoration upgrade (return visit for painting) | 45 min | +A$75 |

### Staff Training

No formal qualification required. 1-day training covers technique and safety:
- Australasian Doula College offers belly casting workshops
- Practice on exercise ball or silicone belly prosthetic before first client
- Any staff member can be trained

### Safety Notes

- Plaster generates mild heat during setting — disclose before starting
- Not before 28 weeks or after 38 weeks
- Check for latex allergy — use latex-free plaster bandage
- Ensure client is hydrated; offer water after

## 6. Women-Focused Space Policy

GTT Center Perth is a **women's safe space**.

**Service areas (treatment rooms, lounge):** Women only.

**Partners and visitors:** Welcome in the cafe/partner seating area near the entrance only. Not in treatment rooms or main lounge.
- Signage: "Patient lounge — quiet zone for our guests. Partners and visitors: please use the cafe area near reception."
- Partner experience: comfortable seating, WiFi, phone charging, snacks and drinks

**Children:** Recommended not to attend (self-care environment).
