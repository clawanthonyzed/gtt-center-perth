## 2. THE COMMERCIAL MODEL QUESTION

This is the only question that must be decided before any numbers are finalised.

### Option A — Venue/Experience Fee + Room Rent (RECOMMENDED)
GTT Center Perth charges a fixed **GTT Experience Fee** per visit.  
Subtenants (massage, nails, brows, scan) pay **weekly room/chair rent**.  
Customers pay subtenants **directly** for their individual services.

**Money flow:**
```
Customer → pays GTT Center Perth → A$120 experience fee
Customer → pays massage therapist directly → A$90–110
Customer → pays nail tech directly → A$70–90
Subtenant → pays GTT Center Perth → A$150–300/week room rent
```

**What A$120 covers:** GTT coordination, lounge access, premium snacks (coconut water, protein bars, herbal teas), parking, booking management, fasting instruction reminders, pathology coordination.

**Why recommended:**
- Legally cleanest — GTT Center Perth is a venue, not a beauty employer
- Subtenants are unambiguously independent (own pricing, own clients, own ABN)
- Zero sham contracting risk (2024 Closing Loopholes Act)
- Operationally simple — no per-session splits, no service payroll
- Room rent = guaranteed income floor before a single customer walks in

---

### Option B — Full Package Sold by GTT Center Perth
GTT Center Perth sells the complete experience (A$200–360), collects all revenue, pays subtenants a session rate.

**Why not recommended (for now):**
- GTT Center Perth must pay subtenants per session → absorbs service cost risk
- If subtenants also pay room rent, they will renegotiate (double-dipping friction)
- Session fees + control over services = sham contracting risk under "whole of relationship" test
- Higher complexity: GST, BAS, per-session invoices from each subtenant
- Revisit at 6+ months once volume is proven — may be worth switching to full ownership model then

## 7. BREAK-EVEN ANALYSIS

**Fixed monthly costs:** A$10,816  
**Contribution from room rent:** A$4,006 (certain income once subtenants signed)  
**Remaining to cover from experience fees:** A$6,810  
**Break-even visits:** A$6,810 ÷ A$120 = **57 visits/month = 13 visits/week = ~3/day**

3 customers per day breaks even. This is the floor.

| Visits/month | Revenue | Costs | Profit |
|---|---|---|---|
| 57 (break-even) | A$10,850 | A$10,816 | A$0 |
| 64 (conservative) | A$12,086 | A$10,816 | +A$1,270 |
| 96 (base) | A$16,226 | A$10,816 | +A$5,410 |
| 128 (strong) | A$21,943 | A$11,500 | +A$10,443 |

**Risk profile is low:** Room rent alone covers ~37% of monthly costs before a single customer visits. Break-even is 3 customers/day — well within range in Week 1 with midwife referrals.
