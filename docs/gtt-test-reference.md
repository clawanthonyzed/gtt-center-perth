## 1. WHAT IS THE GTT?

The **Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT)**, commonly called the GTT, is the gold-standard screening test for **Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM)** — a form of diabetes that develops during pregnancy.

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| Full name | 75g Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT) |
| Common names | GTT, GDM test, gestational diabetes test, glucose tolerance test |
| Standard body | Australasian Diabetes in Pregnancy Society (ADIPS) |
| Current guideline | ADIPS 2025 Consensus Recommendations (supersedes ADIPS 2014 — endorsed by AACB, RCPA, RANZCOG, ADS, ACM) |
| Glucose load | 75 grams |
| Duration on-site | 2–2.5 hours |
| Blood draws | 3 (fasting, 1-hour, 2-hour) |

## 2. WHO NEEDS THE TEST AND WHEN

### Universal screening (all pregnant women)
- **24–28 weeks gestation** — offered to ALL pregnant women in Australia
- WA: performed at PathWest, WDP, and accredited collection centres

### Early testing (high-risk women — before 24 weeks)
Ordered earlier if any of:
- Previous GDM in a prior pregnancy
- Pre-diabetes or impaired fasting glucose (HbA1c 5.7–6.4% pre-pregnancy)
- BMI ≥30 at booking
- Family history of type 2 diabetes (first-degree relative)
- Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
- Certain ethnicities: Aboriginal/Torres Strait Islander, Asian, South Asian, Middle Eastern, Pacific Islander
- Macrosomic baby in previous pregnancy (birth weight > 4.5kg)
- Stillbirth or perinatal loss in previous pregnancy

**Implication for GTT Center Perth:**  
High-risk women may present as early as 10–16 weeks. The same test procedure applies — same clinical requirements. These patients are often MORE anxious (because they've been flagged as high-risk) and benefit most from a premium waiting experience.

### Who does NOT get the GTT
- Women who have already been diagnosed with pre-existing diabetes (Type 1 or Type 2)
- Women who are managing established GDM from a previous pregnancy via ongoing monitoring
- Women who physician has determined are not suitable (medical decision — not GTT Center Perth's call)

## 3. PATIENT PREPARATION REQUIREMENTS

### Fasting
- **Duration:** Minimum 8 hours, maximum 14 hours (overnight fast recommended)
- **Water:** Permitted and encouraged — hydration improves vein access for blood draws
- **Prohibited during fast:**
  - All food (including mints, chewing gum, lollies — even small amounts affect results)
  - All beverages except plain water (no coffee, tea, juice, soft drink, flavoured water, milk)
  - Cigarettes and nicotine products
  - Non-essential medications (patient should check with their GP — some medications affect glucose)

### Day of the test
- No exercise on the morning of the test (exercise alters glucose uptake)
- Wear comfortable, loose clothing (arms accessible for 3 venepunctures)
- Bring pathology request form (from GP or midwife)
- Bring Medicare card
- Arrive FASTING — test cannot begin if patient has eaten

### Start time requirement — CRITICAL OPERATIONAL CONSTRAINT

> **ADIPS 2025 Guideline:** The OGTT should commence between **8:00am and 10:00am**.

**Why this constraint exists:**
- Glucose tolerance has a circadian (time-of-day) rhythm regulated by cortisol and other hormones
- Morning cortisol rises, affecting insulin sensitivity in a predictable pattern
- The ADIPS 2025 diagnostic thresholds (fasting ≥5.3, 1hr ≥10.6, 2hr ≥9.0) were established using tests that started before 10am
- An afternoon test may produce different results that cannot be accurately compared to these reference ranges
- Clinicians and pathology labs require the test to start ≤10am for results to be diagnostically valid

**GTT Center Perth implication:**
- Last GTT start time: **9:30am** (to ensure T=120 draw by 11:30am)
- Afternoon shift: wellness services ONLY — no GTT testing (medically inappropriate)
- This is NOT a limitation — it's a structural feature that makes the morning slots scarce and premium

## 4. TEST PROCEDURE — STEP BY STEP

### Pre-test (Phlebotomist, before patient arrives)
1. Confirm patient's GTT start time in Fresha
2. Prepare labelling supplies: 3 × grey-top vacutainers (fluoride-oxalate preservative) per patient
3. Prepare glucose solution:
   - 75g glucose powder (Polycal or equivalent) dissolved in 250–300mL water
   - Mix immediately before use (or use pre-prepared commercial glucose drink)
   - Temperature: room temperature or slightly cool (cold is unpalatable; too warm may cause nausea)
4. Confirm refrigerator is at 2–8°C and temperature log is current

### Step 1 — Patient Arrival and Identification
- Receptionist confirms fasting status verbally: "When did you last eat or drink anything except water?"
  - If < 8 hours: DO NOT proceed. Consult Imara. Reschedule.
  - If ≥ 8 hours: proceed
- Collect pathology request form — confirm patient name, DOB, gestational age, requesting doctor
- Confirm correct patient (name + DOB verbal check)
- Escort to collection room

### Step 2 — Fasting Blood Draw (T=0)
**Tube type:** Grey-top vacutainer (sodium fluoride/potassium oxalate — prevents glycolysis)

**Procedure:**
1. Patient seated (reclining chair preferred for pregnant patient)
2. Confirm patient comfort, offer to have patient lie down if preferred
3. Apply tourniquet, locate vein (antecubital fossa preferred)
4. Skin prep: 70% isopropyl alcohol wipe, air dry 30 seconds
5. Insert needle (21–23G butterfly or straight needle)
6. Collect blood (3–4mL minimum for glucose analysis)
7. Remove tourniquet before withdrawing needle
8. Apply pressure, cotton wool/bandage
9. IMMEDIATELY:
   - Label tube: patient name, DOB, date, time of collection, collector ID
   - Record exact draw time in collection log
   - Mix tube gently (8–10 inversions — do NOT shake)
10. Place in cool pack/refrigerator

### Step 3 — Administer Glucose Drink (immediately after T=0 draw)
1. Hand patient the prepared 75g glucose drink
2. Instruct: "Please drink all of this within 5 minutes. Try to drink it steadily."
3. Record the time the patient FINISHES the drink — this is T=0 (clinical)
4. If patient vomits the drink within 30 minutes: test is invalid. Cannot restart same day. Reschedule.
5. If patient vomits PARTIALLY: flag to referring doctor. Collection may continue but results noted.

**Glucose drink alternatives:**
- Standard: Polycal glucose powder in 250mL water
- Pre-made options: Glucola (orange or fruit punch flavour)
- 75g glucose is NON-NEGOTIABLE — there is no substitute quantity or food-based alternative

### Step 4 — Service Window (T=0 to T=120)
Patient may:
- Receive any booked wellness services
- Use the lounge
- Rest quietly

Patient may NOT:
- Exercise
- Eat or drink (except water)
- Smoke
- Leave the premises

### Step 5 — 1-Hour Blood Draw (T=60)
- **Time:** 60 minutes from when patient finished the glucose drink (not from when they arrived)
- **Tolerance:** ±5 minutes (i.e., draw between 55–65 minutes)
- **Tube type:** Grey-top vacutainer (same as fasting)
- **Procedure:** Same as T=0 draw
- Per the no-interrupt rule (`operations-manual.md`), services are scheduled in the gaps between draws so this should not arise. If a client is running behind schedule, complete the draw in the lounge/reception area — do not pull a client out of an active service.
- Label: include "1 hour" designation, exact time, patient ID

### Step 6 — 2-Hour Blood Draw (T=120)
- **Time:** 120 minutes from when patient finished the glucose drink
- **Tolerance:** ±10 minutes
- **Procedure:** Same as T=0 and T=60
- Label: include "2 hour" designation, exact time, patient ID
- After this draw: patient may leave
- Offer snacks IMMEDIATELY after final draw (patient has been fasting 10+ hours)

### Step 7 — Post-Collection Specimen Handling
1. All three labelled tubes placed in biohazard specimen bag with completed pathology request form
2. Seal bag
3. Place in cool pack (samples stable at 2–8°C for up to 6 hours)
4. Dispatch to WDP/PathWest lab via agreed courier/transport
5. Complete dispatch log: time dispatched, patient ID, number of tubes
6. Retain dispatch log (7 years)

### Step 8 — Results
- Lab analyses: plasma glucose for each time point
- Results transmitted electronically to referring doctor (same day or next business day)
- **GTT Center Perth does NOT receive results**
- **GTT Center Perth does NOT communicate results to patients**
- Patient is instructed to follow up with their GP or midwife for results

## 5. DIAGNOSTIC THRESHOLDS (ADIPS 2025 CONSENSUS)

> **Updated:** ADIPS 2025 supersedes ADIPS 2014. Thresholds have changed. All GTT Center Perth clinical materials use ADIPS 2025 values only.

GDM is diagnosed if **ANY ONE** of the following values is met or exceeded:

| Time point | **ADIPS 2025 threshold** | ADIPS 2014 (superseded) |
|---|---|---|
| Fasting | **≥ 5.3 mmol/L** | ~~≥ 5.1 mmol/L~~ |
| 1 hour | **≥ 10.6 mmol/L** | ~~≥ 10.0 mmol/L~~ |
| 2 hours | **≥ 9.0 mmol/L** | ~~≥ 8.5 mmol/L~~ |

**Impaired fasting glucose (IFG):** Fasting 5.3–6.9 mmol/L — GDM threshold is ≥5.3 by ADIPS 2025.

**Pre-existing diabetes (not GDM):**
- Fasting ≥7.0 mmol/L or 2hr ≥11.1 mmol/L → likely pre-existing T2DM — refer urgently

**GDM prevalence:** Approximately 12–18% of tested women will receive a GDM diagnosis.  
At GTT Center Perth: **~1 in 6 patients** will receive a positive result. Patients are referred back to their GP/midwife for GDM management — no in-house dietitian at this stage (`services-pricing-locked.md` Future Services).

## 6. COLLECTION ROOM REQUIREMENTS

### ACSQHC (Australian Commission on Safety and Quality in Health Care) Standards

**Room specifications:**
- Size: minimum 10 sqm (12–15 sqm preferred for pregnant patient comfort)
- Patient chair: adjustable recliner with armrest (NOT a standard waiting chair)
- Phlebotomist workspace: bench at appropriate height, good lighting
- Privacy: closed door, no external windows into room
- Accessibility: 900mm minimum door clearance (wheelchair/pram accessible)
- Temperature: climate controlled (2–8°C not required for room, only for specimen fridge)

**Essential equipment:**
| Item | Standard / Requirement |
|---|---|
| Sharps container | Approved (Daniels Healthcare UltiMed or equivalent), < 2/3 full when replaced |
| Biohazard waste bin | Yellow lid, biohazard symbol |
| Specimen refrigerator | 2–8°C, with minimum/maximum thermometer, daily temperature log |
| Hand hygiene station | Soap + water basin AND ABHR (alcohol-based hand rub) at point of care |
| Personal protective equipment | Gloves (non-latex preferred), apron |
| Emergency call | Intercom or call button to reception |

**Required signage:**
- Biohazard symbol on room door
- "Restricted entry when in use" sign
- Hand hygiene reminder at sink

**Consumables stocked per shift:**
- Vacutainers: grey-top (fluoride-oxalate) — 3 per patient + spares
- Needles: 21G and 23G butterfly sets, straight needles
- Tourniquets (single-use preferred in clinical setting)
- Alcohol prep pads (70% isopropyl)
- Cotton balls / gauze
- Medical tape / bandages
- Glucose drink (75g per patient, room temperature)
- Biohazard specimen bags
- Request form copies

## 7. PHLEBOTOMIST QUALIFICATIONS AND REQUIREMENTS

### Minimum qualification (Australia)
**Certificate III in Pathology Collection — HLT37215**

| Details | Value |
|---|---|
| AQF level | Certificate III |
| Duration | 6–12 months (part-time study + clinical placement hours) |
| Clinical placement hours required | Minimum 120 hours in a clinical setting |
| Units included | Venepuncture, capillary collection, GTT procedure, specimen handling, patient ID, infection control, chain of custody |
| Governing framework | Health Training Package (HLT) |
| AHPRA registration required? | **NO** — phlebotomists are NOT regulated by AHPRA |
| Renewal requirement | No formal expiry — but CPD recommended (AIMS/AACB guidelines) |

### Higher qualifications (preferred)
- **Certificate IV in Laboratory Techniques** — HLT47315 (more comprehensive, includes more complex specimens)
- **Enrolled Nurse (EN)** or **Registered Nurse (RN)** — can perform venepuncture as part of nursing scope
- **Diploma of Nursing** — includes pathology collection competency

### NATA (National Association of Testing Authorities) Requirement
- **GTT Center Perth cannot perform collections independently** without a NATA framework
- Options:
  1. **NATA-accredited collection centre (LCC):** GTT Center Perth applies to become a Licensed Collection Centre under WDP or PathWest's NATA accreditation
     - Process: 3–6 months for approval
     - WDP explicitly has a "Partner with us" program for this
     - Phlebotomist works under WDP's NATA umbrella
  2. **Full NATA accreditation for GTT Center Perth:** Own lab accreditation
     - Complex, expensive, not necessary — avoid for launch
     - Only consider if GTT Center Perth processes specimens in-house (no plan for this)

**Recommended:** Option 1 — WDP Licensed Collection Centre. This is the model confirmed.

### Continuing Professional Development (CPD)
No formal annual renewal for Cert III, BUT phlebotomists must maintain:
- Current CPR (HLTAID011) — renew every 3 years
- Anaphylaxis training — renew annually
- Up-to-date knowledge of WDP/PathWest protocols (provided by partner during onboarding and annual review)

### Background checks required
- National Police Clearance (renewed every 3 years)
- Working with Vulnerable People check (WA requirement when working with pregnant women)

## 10. PATIENT SAFETY DURING THE GTT

### Common adverse events

| Event | Frequency | Response |
|---|---|---|
| Nausea after glucose drink | Common (10–30%) | Sit quietly, cool water, do NOT give food |
| Vomiting of glucose drink | Uncommon (~5%) | Cannot restart today — reschedule with referring doctor |
| Vasovagal (fainting/near-faint) | Occasional | Lie patient flat, elevate legs, DO NOT leave |
| Difficult venepuncture | Occasional | Max 2 attempts per arm, see protocol |
| Hypoglycemia during test | Rare | If T=120 completed: can give food. If not: consult referring doctor |
| Patient refuses to complete test | Rare | Document, do not pressure — their decision |

### Why GTT Center Perth reduces adverse events
- Comfortable reclining chairs (vs hard plastic pathology chairs)
- Massage during the wait reduces stress/anxiety (cortisol reduction)
- Hydration encouraged
- Warm, calm environment vs clinical pathology atmosphere
- Staff trained in maternal-specific patient management

## 12. GTT CENTER PERTH CLINICAL POSITIONING

**GTT Center Perth is NOT a pathology laboratory.**  
**GTT Center Perth IS a venue that facilitates pathology collection as a service component.**

Specifically:
- GTT Center Perth provides the venue, equipment, phlebotomist employment, and booking management
- WDP/PathWest provides the NATA accreditation umbrella, specimen processing, and result reporting
- GTT Center Perth does not interpret, hold, or communicate results
- GTT Center Perth does not make any clinical decisions about the GTT

**Marketing language (mandatory):**
- ✓ "We host your GTT appointment while you enjoy our services"
- ✓ "Our phlebotomist collects your blood samples as part of the GTT process"
- ✗ "We perform GTT testing" (implies independent diagnostic capability)
- ✗ "Get your GTT results with us" (we don't hold results)
- ✗ "Our GTT test is [X standard]" (we don't own the test — the pathology lab does)
