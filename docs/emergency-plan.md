## 5. PATIENT MEDICAL EMERGENCIES

### 5.1 Vasovagal (Fainting / Near-Fainting)

**Trigger:** Patient looks pale, dizzy, sweaty, loses consciousness during or after blood draw.

**Response:**
1. **STOP the draw immediately** (remove needle if in progress)
2. Lay patient flat on the reclining chair or floor — elevate legs above heart level
3. **Do NOT leave the patient**
4. Call for Imara (or designated first aider) immediately
5. Loosen any tight clothing
6. Offer cool, damp cloth to forehead if available
7. Monitor: breathing, colour, responsiveness
8. If unconscious and not breathing: **call 000, begin CPR, get AED**
9. If conscious and recovering: remain lying flat for minimum 10 minutes before sitting
10. Do not proceed with T=60 or T=120 draws until Imara approves
11. Document in incident register within 2 hours

**Tell 000:** "We have a pregnant woman who has fainted at [venue address]. She is [X] weeks pregnant and has been undergoing a glucose tolerance test. She is now [conscious/unconscious] and [breathing/not breathing]."

---

### 5.2 Vomiting of Glucose Drink

**Trigger:** Patient vomits within 30 minutes of finishing the glucose drink.

**Response:**
1. Assist patient to bathroom or provide basin immediately
2. Do not pressure patient to continue
3. Inform phlebotomist immediately
4. The test **cannot be restarted today** (glucose dose cannot be re-administered — invalid test)
5. Contact patient's referring doctor's practice: advise patient needs to reschedule
6. Provide patient with snacks and water (fast must end after test is aborted)
7. Refund or reschedule appointment per cancellation policy
8. Document in Fresha notes: "Test aborted — patient vomited glucose drink at T+[X] minutes"

---

### 5.3 Patient Collapses / Loses Consciousness

**Response:**
1. Do not leave patient
2. Call out for help: "HELP — patient down in [location]"
3. Call 000 immediately
4. Check for breathing and pulse
5. If not breathing: begin CPR (30 compressions : 2 breaths), get AED
6. If breathing: place in recovery position (left lateral position — standard for pregnant patients)
7. Receptionist meets ambulance at front door and guides to patient location
8. Do NOT move patient unless in immediate physical danger
9. Tell ambulance: gestational age, last food/water, time of last blood draw, any known medical conditions from intake form
10. Notify WDP/PathWest clinical liaison same day
11. Notify solicitor same day
12. Complete incident register within 2 hours

---

### 5.4 Patient Has Signs of Pre-Eclampsia / Obstetric Emergency

**Symptoms:** Sudden severe headache, visual disturbance (flashing lights, blurred vision), severe abdominal pain, rapidly swelling face/hands, patient reports decreased fetal movement.

**Response:**
1. **Do NOT treat. Call 000 immediately.**
2. Keep patient calm, lying on her left side
3. Do not give food or water
4. Tell 000: "Pregnant woman with [symptoms], approximately [X] weeks pregnant, at [venue address]"
5. Notify Imara
6. Do not delay 000 call for any reason — obstetric emergencies require ambulance

---

### 5.5 Adverse Reaction During Massage / Beauty Service

**Trigger:** Patient reports dizziness, shortness of breath, chest pain, severe itching, rash, swelling, or difficulty breathing.

**Response:**
1. **Stop the service immediately**
2. Assist patient to lie flat
3. If anaphylaxis suspected (rapid swelling, difficulty breathing): **call 000 immediately**
4. Use EpiPen if available and patient consents (confirm allergy history first)
5. If mild reaction (itching, redness): stop service, remove product, wash area, monitor for 15 minutes
6. Never resume service if any systemic reaction is occurring
7. Advise patient to follow up with GP
8. Document in incident register

## 6. FIRE / BUILDING EMERGENCY

### Evacuation Procedure

1. **Activate fire alarm** (if alarm exists) or call out loudly: "EVACUATE NOW"
2. Staff immediately assist patients out of treatment rooms and collection room
3. **GTT patients in collection room: phlebotomist removes any needle in progress, applies pressure bandage, escorts to exit**
4. Do not stop to collect belongings
5. Guide all patients to assembly point: [to be inserted]
6. Call 000 from outside the building: "Fire at [address]. We have [number] pregnant patients evacuating."
7. Account for all staff and patients at assembly point
8. Do NOT re-enter building until fire brigade says it is safe

**Fire wardens:** Imara (primary) | Receptionist (secondary)

## 7. DATA BREACH

1. Imara assesses: is patient health information involved?
2. If yes: notify solicitor within 24 hours before taking any further action
3. Under Notifiable Data Breaches scheme (Privacy Act): if breach likely to cause serious harm → notify OAIC within 30 days
4. Do NOT notify patients before getting legal advice (wording must be approved by solicitor)
5. Document: what data, how many patients, what happened, what was done in response

## 9. INCIDENT REGISTER

All incidents, near-misses, and adverse events must be recorded in the incident register within 24 hours.

**Record:**
- Date, time, location
- Patient/staff name (confidential — separate from public records)
- Nature of incident
- Response taken
- Outcome
- Follow-up required
- Name of person completing the record

**Store:** Secure, confidential. 7 years minimum.

**WorkSafe WA notification:** Notifiable incidents (worker incapacity, dangerous occurrence, fatality) must be reported to WorkSafe WA **as soon as practicable**. Phone: 1300 307 877.
