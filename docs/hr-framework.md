## 2. NATIONAL EMPLOYMENT STANDARDS (NES) — SUMMARY

All employees receive at minimum:
1. **Maximum weekly hours:** 38 ordinary hours (part-time: pro-rated)
2. **Flexible working:** right to request (parental, carer, disability, 55+)
3. **Parental leave:** up to 12 months unpaid (after 12 months service)
4. **Annual leave:** 4 weeks per year (pro-rated for part-time)
5. **Personal/carer's leave:** 10 days per year (personal illness + carer's)
6. **Compassionate leave:** 2 days per bereavement event
7. **Family & domestic violence leave:** 10 days paid per year (all employees from Day 1)
8. **Community service leave:** unpaid (jury duty, emergency management)
9. **Long service leave:** WA: 8.67 weeks after 7 years
10. **Public holidays:** paid (if ordinary working day falls on public holiday)
11. **Notice and redundancy pay:** based on length of service

## 3. EMPLOYMENT CONTRACTS — REQUIREMENTS

Each employee receives a written contract before Day 1 containing:
- Full name, role title, classification under award
- Employment type (permanent part-time or casual)
- Ordinary hours per week and days of work
- Base rate of pay (must be at or above award rate — confirm current award rate before issuing)
- Pay period (weekly)
- Superannuation fund details
- Leave entitlements (reference NES + award)
- Probationary period (3 months — during which notice period is 1 week)
- Notice periods (post-probation):
  - Employee to employer: 2 weeks (Hair & Beauty / Clerks / HPSS awards)
  - Employer to employee: scales with service length (see NES)
- Termination conditions
- Confidentiality clause (patient privacy, business information)
- Intellectual property clause (any client lists, booking data stays with GTT Center Perth)

**Engage Perth employment solicitor to draft template contracts for each role.** Budget A$1,500–2,500 for templates.

## 5. SUPERANNUATION

**Rate:** 11.5% of Ordinary Time Earnings (OTE) from 1 July 2025. Rising to 12% from 1 July 2025.  
*(Confirm current rate with accountant at hire — rate increases annually.)*

**Payment:** Super must be paid at least quarterly; monthly via Xero preferred (cash flow better, avoids large quarterly hits).

**Fund choice:** Offer employees a choice of fund. If they don't choose within 2 weeks, use the employer's default fund. Confirm default fund with accountant.

**STP (Single Touch Payroll):** Super payments reported through STP Phase 2 in Xero. Not optional.

## 7. LEAVE MANAGEMENT

### Annual Leave
- Part-time employees: 4 weeks pro-rata (e.g., 25hrs/wk = 100 hours annual leave per year = 8.33 hours per month)
- Requests: minimum 2 weeks notice (ordinary leave); Imara approves
- Peak period restrictions: December, January (holiday season — may be busier)
- Cannot force employee to take leave except in specific circumstances (discuss with solicitor if needed)

### Personal/Carer's Leave
- 10 days per year, pro-rated for part-time
- First day of new leave year: first day of employment anniversary
- Employee must notify before shift start (or as soon as practicable)
- Carer's certificate or statutory declaration required for absences > 1 day
- Imara covers or arranges cover for sick leave

### Family & Domestic Violence Leave
- 10 days paid from Day 1 (2024 NES change — ALL employees including part-time and casual)
- Confidential — do not record specifics in leave records
- Employee provides evidence where reasonable (does not have to be police report — can be statutory declaration)

### Parental Leave
- 12 months unpaid after 12 months service (NES)
- Government paid parental leave (Dad and Partner Pay, etc.) separate to employer obligations
- Right to return to same or equivalent role after leave
- Key risk for GTT Center Perth: pregnant employee — plan for leave coverage

## 8. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

### Process
1. **Verbal warning** (documented in Imara's notes): first occurrence of minor issues
2. **Written warning** (formal letter via Fern template): repeated or more serious issues
3. **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)**: 30-day plan with specific measurable targets
4. **Termination** (if PIP not met, or serious misconduct): following process below

### Termination Process (for performance/conduct)
- **Never terminate on the spot** (except summary dismissal for serious misconduct)
- Show cause letter: outline reasons, give employee opportunity to respond (minimum 24 hours)
- Imara meets employee with a support person (employee's right)
- Decision made after hearing response
- Final pay: includes all outstanding wages, leave, and any redundancy (if applicable)
- Notice period served or paid out
- Engage employment solicitor for any termination — unfair dismissal risk is real

### Summary Dismissal (Serious Misconduct)
Can dismiss without notice or pay in lieu only for:
- Theft or fraud
- Serious safety breach
- Patient abuse
- Serious harassment or discrimination
- Gross negligence resulting in harm

Even in these cases: document thoroughly, seek solicitor advice before acting.

### Unfair Dismissal Risk
Employees are eligible for unfair dismissal claims after:
- 6 months service (businesses with 15+ employees)
- 12 months service (businesses with < 15 employees — GTT Center Perth threshold at launch)

GTT Center Perth starts with < 15 employees. 12-month protection period applies.

## 9. WORK HEALTH AND SAFETY (WHS)

### GTT Center Perth WHS Obligations (Work Health and Safety Act 2020 WA)
- Maintain a safe workplace (primary duty of care)
- Consult with workers on WHS matters
- Train workers in health and safety relevant to their role
- Maintain records of incidents and hazards

### Specific Hazards
| Hazard | Control measure |
|---|---|
| Sharps (needles, vacutainers) | Approved sharps containers, phlebotomist training, no recapping needles |
| Nail salon chemicals (acrylic monomer, solvents) | LEV (local exhaust ventilation) mandatory, PPE for nail tech, pregnancy-safe products only |
| Manual handling (massage tables, equipment) | Correct lifting technique training, table height adjustment |
| Biological waste | Biohazard bins, trained phlebotomist, WDP/PathWest collection for bio-waste |
| Slips/trips | Non-slip flooring in treatment areas, wet floor protocol |
| Electrical (nail lamp, equipment) | Annual electrical safety inspection |

### Incident Reporting
- Notifiable incidents to WorkSafe WA (worker incapacity, dangerous incident, fatality): must notify ASAP
- Internal incident register: record all incidents/near-misses within 24 hours
- Workers compensation claim: submitted via WorkCover WA if employee injured at work

### WorkCover WA
- **Register as employer with WorkCover WA before first hire**
- Workers compensation insurance: required from Day 1 of employment
- Premium: based on wages and industry code. Budget ~5% of wages = ~A$800/month at launch
