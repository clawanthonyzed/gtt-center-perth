## S1. GTT Test — First 10-15 Minutes (Confirmed)

**Question:** Is 10-15 minutes from patient arrival to "seated in lounge" achievable? Can services start at T+15?

**Answer: YES — confirmed.**

**Breakdown:**
- Check-in + consent: ~2 minutes
- Walk to collection room, seat patient: ~1 minute
- Phlebotomist 2-identifier check: ~1 minute
- Fasting blood draw (3 tubes): ~5-7 minutes
- Glucose drink (patient finishes within 5 minutes): ~3-5 minutes
- **Total T=0 to seated in lounge: 12-16 minutes**
- T+15 as a service start time is fully achievable

**Protocol check — "remain at rest":**
The GTT protocol requires patients to remain "seated and at rest" during the 2-hour wait. This means:
- No exercise, no vigorous walking
- No eating (except water and non-caffeinated herbal tea)
- NO caffeinated drinks

"At rest" does NOT mean lying flat. It means avoiding cardiovascular exertion. A reclined massage table or treatment chair with patient in a supported position is clinically compatible with "seated at rest."

**Services compatible from T+15:** Massage (reclined), nails (seated, hands/feet), brows (semi-reclined), facial (reclined), 3D scan (semi-reclined). Patient must return for 1-hour draw at T+60-75 (5 minutes) and 2-hour draw at T+120 (5 minutes).

**Practical service scheduling:**
- T+15: Service 1 begins
- T+70: Service 1 must end (leaves buffer before T+75 draw)
- T+80: Service 2 can begin
- T+110: Service 2 must end (leaves buffer before T+120 draw)
- **Effective service window:** 55 minutes pre-draw + 30 minutes post-first-draw = 85 usable minutes

**Can patients do 2 services?**
Yes — example combinations:
- Brow wax+tint (25 min) + gel manicure (45 min) = 70 min — fits
- Express facial (30 min) + 3D scan (25 min) = 55 min — fits
- Pregnancy massage (45 min) + blowdry (30 min) = 75 min — tight; use separate slots around T+75 draw
- Brow wax (20 min) + pregnancy massage (45 min) = 65 min — fits well

## S3. Receptionist / Manager Role — Award and Pay Clarified

**Award:** Hair and Beauty Industry Award MA000005 is the correct award for a wellness/day spa receptionist/manager role.
**Level for pure reception:** MA000005 Level 1-2 ($26.55-$27.50/hr base)
**Level for receptionist + manager (supervises staff):** MA000005 Level 5-6 (~$29.49-$32.00/hr base)
**Casual rate at Level 5-6:** ~$36.86-$40.00/hr (base + 25%)

**Pay premium vs pure reception:** Approximately $3-6/hr more = A$115-230/week more on a 38-hour week.

**Recommended approach:** Hire at Level 3-4 initially (they grow into the manager role). Classify as Level 5 once managing 2+ staff independently.

## S4. Staff Model Implications (Confirmed Change from Room Rental)

**Previous research.md recommendation:** Room rental model (subtenants)
**Confirmed model (Day 48):** Staff employment model (casual/part-time employees)

**Why this changes the financials:**
- Room rental: revenue = room rent income only (A$150-350/practitioner/week)
- Staff model: revenue = 100% of service fees; cost = Award wages + super

**Room rental was lower risk at launch.** Staff model generates higher revenue but requires higher breakeven.

**Hybrid recommendation for launch:**
- Employ: massage therapists, nail techs, hairdressers, beauty therapists (under staff model)
- Subtenant: Bloom Baby (3D scan) — zero capex, proven quality
- Contractor: Dietitian (APD own Medicare provider number; room hire arrangement)
- Contractor: Lactation consultant (IBCLC — Month 4+)

This hybrid captures the revenue upside of the staff model while mitigating risk on the higher-capital services (3D scanner) and regulated services (dietitian/lactation).

## S7. China Sourcing — TGA Compliance Summary

See hire-purchase-china.md for full detail.

**TGA-regulated (CANNOT import direct from China without registration):**
- Ultrasound machines (Class IIa/IIb) — must buy from TGA-registered Australian distributor
- TENS machines (Class IIa) — must be ARTG-listed before commercial hire/sale

**NOT TGA-regulated (can import from China freely):**
- Exercise/birth balls, peanut balls, birth combs, acupressure balls, inflatable birth pools
- Spray tan booth hardware (general electrical safety rules apply)
- Furniture, equipment that is not therapeutic

**DHA spray tan solution:** Regulated by AICIS (cosmetic ingredient notification), not TGA or APVMA.

## S14. Spray Tan — Pregnancy Safety Protocol (Confirmed)

**From second trimester:**
- DHA is topically low-risk
- Main concern: inhalation of spray mist in enclosed booth
- Mandatory protocol: nose/mouth filter, eye protection, lip balm barrier
- Signed informed consent acknowledging inhalation risk
- Strong ventilation (8-10 air changes/hour minimum)

**NOT recommended on GTT day** for pregnant clients (glucose drink + spray mist inhalation risk together is not best practice). Offer as next-visit or standalone afternoon booking.

**For non-pregnant clients:** Spray tan available any time with no restrictions.
