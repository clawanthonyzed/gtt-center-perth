## ⚠ FINDING — Saturday AM GTT Runs at a Loss on Direct Labor Alone

Before the tables: once the **confirmed** MA000005 Saturday penalty (133% permanent / 150% casual) is applied to the 8 treatment staff, Saturday AM GTT does not cover its own direct labor cost at the current 10-client/A$250 model — **even before adding weekend reception coverage.**

| | Optimistic (phlebotomist ordinary-hours carve-out applies, unconfirmed) | Conservative (full 150% penalty on phlebotomists too) |
|---|---|---|
| Saturday AM Revenue | A$2,500 | A$2,500 |
| Saturday AM Direct Labor | A$3,127 | A$3,290 |
| **Saturday AM Contribution** | **-A$627/day** | **-A$790/day** |

**Why:** the same staff (2 phlebotomists + 8 treatment) must be present regardless of exact client count — reducing Saturday volume doesn't fix this, since the concurrency requirement means labor cost is largely fixed while revenue would drop with fewer clients, making it worse. The real levers are: a Saturday price premium, a leaner Saturday treatment roster (fewer than 2-per-line, trading capacity for cost), or accepting Saturday AM as a deliberate loss-leader for demand-building in the early months. **This needs your decision — I haven't picked one for you.**

**Saturday PM (standalone) stays positive** even with the penalty (+A$424/day) — PM's hours-based model has more cushion. It's specifically Saturday AM GTT that's the problem.

The monthly/quarterly/yearly tables below still show the whole business solidly profitable — weekday operations (boosted by the 07:00 start) carry the month. But Saturday AM, in isolation, is a net drag, not a contributor, under current assumptions.
