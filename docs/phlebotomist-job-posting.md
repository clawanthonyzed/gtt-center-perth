### About GTT Center Perth

GTT Center Perth is Western Australia's first dedicated Glucose Tolerance Test (GTT) wellness lounge — opening [Month] 2026. We've reimagined the mandatory 2.5-hour GTT wait by pairing clinical-grade blood collection with premium pregnancy wellness services (massage, nails, brows, 3D scan).

This is not a standard pathology lab. Our patients arrive anxious and fasting and leave relaxed and cared for. You'll be part of a small, purpose-driven team working in a beautiful setting designed specifically for pregnant women.

We operate as a Licensed Collection Centre under [WDP/PathWest]'s NATA accreditation.

---

### Role Overview

As GTT Center Perth's lead phlebotomist, you are the clinical foundation of the entire business. You perform blood collections for the 75g OGTT (T=0, T=60, T=120) across up to 5 concurrent staggered patients each morning, coordinate specimen dispatch to [WDP/PathWest], and ensure our collection room meets ACSQHC and NATA standards at all times.

You will work in a small, collaborative team — mostly alongside the Venue Manager (Managing Director), a receptionist, and wellness practitioners. You are not in a large lab — you are the only phlebotomist on shift (initially), and you own your workspace entirely.

---

### Key Responsibilities

**Clinical (primary)**
- Perform venepuncture (blood collection) for OGTT patients: T=0, T=60, and T=120 draws
- Administer 75g glucose drink with appropriate patient instructions
- Manage up to 5 concurrent staggered OGTT patients using precise time-tracking
- Label and handle all specimens to WDP/PathWest chain-of-custody standards
- Dispatch specimens to the pathology lab via agreed courier/transport schedule
- Maintain the collection room to ACSQHC standards: temperature log, sharps disposal, supply levels
- Complete clinical documentation: collection log, dispatch log, adverse event records
- Monitor patients for adverse events (vasovagal, nausea, vomiting of glucose drink) and manage appropriately
- Never communicate or interpret GTT results — all results go to the referring doctor via [WDP/PathWest]

**Administrative**
- Coordinate daily with receptionist re: patient timing, draw schedules
- Complete WDP/PathWest required documentation per collection session
- Track and reorder supplies at reorder thresholds (glucose solutions, vacutainers, etc.)
- Maintain credential currency: CPR, anaphylaxis training

**Compliance**
- Maintain compliance with NATA Licensed Collection Centre requirements
- Maintain collection room to ACSQHC and WDP/PathWest standards
- Report any specimen rejections or adverse events to the Venue Manager same day

## PART 4 — INTERVIEW PROCESS

### Screening (phone — 15 min)

Conducted by Fern (HR agent preparing questions) / Venue Manager (executing)

Questions:
1. Tell me about your phlebotomy experience. How many years? What settings?
2. Have you performed OGTT/GTT collections before? How many per shift?
3. Are you currently approved as a collector under WDP or PathWest?
4. Why are you interested in a morning-only, part-time role at a wellness venue?
5. Do you have availability for Mon–Fri 7:15am–12:30pm consistently?
6. Are you comfortable managing 4–5 concurrent OGTT patients with staggered draw times?

**Screening pass criteria:** Cert III confirmed, availability confirmed, interest in the venue model genuine, no obvious disqualifying factors.

### In-person interview (1 hour — at venue site or temporary location)

**Interview panel:** Venue Manager

**Assessment areas:**

| Area | Questions |
|---|---|
| Clinical competence | "Walk me through how you manage a patient who feels faint during their T=60 draw while three other patients also need draws in the next 15 minutes." |
| NATA/WDP knowledge | "What does it mean to operate as a Licensed Collection Centre? What are your obligations?" |
| Patient communication | "How do you explain the GTT procedure to a first-time patient who is anxious about needles?" |
| Difficult venepuncture | "What do you do when you can't find a vein after two attempts?" |
| Error handling | "Have you ever made a labelling or timing error? What happened and what did you do?" |
| Team fit | "Describe the ideal workplace culture for you. What makes a great small team?" |
| Schedule reliability | "Describe any situations where you had to miss a shift. How did you handle it?" |

**Practical assessment (if venue is available):** Set up the collection room to ACSQHC standard, demonstrate vacuum tube labelling technique and inversions, demonstrate adverse event response (vasovagal).

### Reference checks (2 required)

- Must include at least one clinical reference (previous employer where they performed venepuncture)
- Ask referee: "Would you re-hire this person?" "How reliable were they for early morning shifts?" "Have they ever had a specimen labelling error or patient adverse event — and how did they handle it?"

### Pre-employment checks

| Check | How | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| National Police Clearance | Police.wa.gov.au (applicant applies) | 2–4 weeks |
| Working with Vulnerable People (WWVP) | WWVP.wa.gov.au (applicant applies) | 1–2 weeks |
| Cert III HLT37215 verification | Request certified copy of certificate | Day of offer |
| CPR currency | Request certificate (HLTAID011, not older than 12 months) | Day of offer |
| Anaphylaxis training | Request certificate (within 12 months) | Day of offer |
| WDP/PathWest credential | WDP confirms applicant is approvable — do this BEFORE making final offer | Week of offer |

**WDP credential check:** Before issuing an employment offer, email or call WDP and confirm the candidate is either (a) already an approved collector under their NATA accreditation, or (b) meets the requirements to be approved. Do not hire someone WDP will reject.

## PART 5 — FALLBACK: SESSIONAL AGENCY COVER

If no direct candidate is hired by **Week 12 (8 weeks before soft open)**:

1. Contact Skilled Medical (skilledmedical.com.au) — specialist healthcare staffing for WA
2. Request: "Sessional phlebotomist, OGTT-experienced, Mon–Fri mornings, [location]"
3. Agency markup: approximately 25–35% on top of hourly rate
4. Agency phlebotomist must STILL meet WDP credential requirements
5. This is a bridge — continue direct recruitment in parallel

**Agency use is more expensive but prevents a delayed opening.** A delayed opening with a 300+ waitlist is a brand risk. The cost of an agency phlebotomist for 3 months (~A$800/month premium vs direct hire) is far less than the cost of a delayed launch.
